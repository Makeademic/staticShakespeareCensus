This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be sold at his shop at the signe of the blacke Beare in Pauls Church-yard"; the original sheet πA2.5 was replaced by a cancel, which is printed on thicker paper and probably dates from 1641, after Thomas Cotes died. [DEEP]

In Folger copy 6, πA1 and πA(πA2) (cancellans title leaf) are mounted.  Leaf 3d4 is patched at the extremity of the outer corners. Port. (on title page) in state 3. Wanting W.T. (2A1 recto-2C2 recto). Leaf l4 is narrow but probably genuine. Imperfections affect text slightly on P6, 2r1, and 3d1 and very slightly on T1, h1, l2, 2c5, 2e3, 2m3, and 3c6. This copy, unusually tall and large-tailed but narrow (especially in the gutters)--text block 35.0 cm. x 23.2 cm.--contains leaves that retain deckle at head, fore-edge, or tail.
