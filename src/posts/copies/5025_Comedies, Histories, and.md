This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The only known private owner of this copy was William Gerhard Mennen (1884-1968). Mennen graduated from Cornell in 1908 with a degree in mechanical engineering and joined the family business. The Mennen Company was established by his father, Gerhard Mennen, and specialized in men’s shaving creams and other toiletries. He took over as president of the company in 1912 and continued to expand the business. He donated a large collection of rare books to his alma mater in 1953, including all four Folios.
