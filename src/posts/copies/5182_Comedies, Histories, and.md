This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

There are annotations throughout the volume numbering from about a dozen to nearly 360 per play, often marking sententiae.\r\n\r\nIn addition to the heavily annotated pages, there are several drawings of manicules and a spear in Timon of Athens. Signature qq1v (the last page of Hamlet) features an annotation reading: 'But I desire the readers mouth to kis the righteous arse.'

The volume is stored in a red clamshell box. The binding is 17th century calfskin with blind tooled fillets. There is a gold-tooled 'D.W.L' on the front board. There are six slightly raised bands on the spine. 'SHAKESPEARE' is written in the second panel. 'LOND. 1623.' appears in the bottom panel. The shelf mark is on circular binder’s leaf paper: 'with A155 Extra'.
