This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The verses by Ben Jonson are of the original impression, but the letterpress of the title page is in 18th century type with Shakespeare's first name misspelled "Wiliam". The portrait by Droeshout, inlaid on the title page, may be from the fourth folio.

This particular copy was once part of the vast library at Cross Hall, the Lancashire residence of a minor branch of the Stanley family related to the Earls of Derby .... Colonel Thomas Stanley evidently acquired the folio c. 1790 .... [T]he copy went to Samuel Christie-Miller of Britwell Court, Buckinghamshire—a Member of Parliament representing Newcastle-under-Lyme in the 1850s who added to the venerable Britwell Library begun by his uncle William Henry Miller. The Shakespeare folio stayed in the family until the estate and library were sold off in a series of auctions held by Sotheby’s over three years .... After passing to New York book dealer, James F. Drake, the copy joined the collection of Mrs. T. Marie Chilton .... In 1960, she gave the folio to the Von der Ahe Library at Loyola Marymount.
