This issue lists William Aspley as the publisher.

From dealer catalogue: "a few small damp stains to margins, 5 tiny holes to the first page of The Comedy of Errors (with slight loss of 3 letters of text), a couple of short closed marginal tears, a little slight soiling and browning .... Lacking the initial leaf, To the Reader, but with all other leaves present and mostly intact. The first 17 and last 19 leaves have been neatly repaired (not recently), with some loss of the ruled border and/or varying degrees of minor text loss to 25 of those leaves, usually only a few words or less to the last few lines. The title and colophon pages have both been laid down, and there is some loss of the imprint at the foot of the title page, including almost all of the date. (The text that remains is: London, Printed by Tho. Cotes, for William Aspley, and are [...] of the Parrat in Pauls Church-yard. 1...). The inner margin of the final leaf has loss of much of the ruled border, portions of each of the last 17 lines of text (usually only a couple of words per line), and the beginning of the two-line colophon: the first word of line one, and part of the initial 'R' from the first word of line two. The five publisher's names and, most importantly, the 1632 date are all intact. The title page portrait of Shakespeare by Martin Droeshout is in the third state, with the tiny difference in the highlighting of the eyes and the extra strand of hair to the right side of the coiffure. The letter 'M' is missing from the artist's name, but the portrait is complete and a fine impression."

Offered for sale by Kay Craddock Antiquarian Bookseller, July 2022, AUS$450,000. Ink inscription on front free endpaper: "To Mrs St George, with affection, Harry." Ink inscription on first page of The Tempest: "Wm Herbert of Swanzy is ye true owner of this booke". Card inserted: "Great is the felicity of  That Man whoes Workes are Just & whoes Desires Are Innocent / Elizabeth Mansell" ; on reverse "A sound heart & A clear Conscience Will endure All Trials but A guilty Conscience Like Glasse will Sweat With The Least Breath" (ZL). From dealer catalogue: "Perhaps the most likely candidate is the politician of that name who was born in 1587. His father, Richard, had settled on an estate near Swansea owned by Richard's wife, so presumably this William Herbert was born there. He was elected Member of Parliament for Cardiff in 1621 and was later Mayor of Cardiff and Constable of Cardiff Castle in the 1630s .... In 1951, Maggs Bros., London, offered this copy of the Second Folio for sale for 205 pounds in their Catalogue 800 (English Literature prior to 1800. PART 7 S-Z). It was purchased by Australian book collector Keith McRoberts, a regular customer of the firm."
