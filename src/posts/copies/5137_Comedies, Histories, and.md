This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

D5r: signature of "James Lakin," c. 18th century. Possibly owned by Thomas Phillipps prior to Horace de Landau. Owned by Landau and inherited by his niece Eugenia Finaly (née Ellenberger), later sold at the Landau library sale at Sotheby's in 12 July 1948 along with F2-F4 as lot 107 to Maggs. Owned between 1948 and 1958 by Dudley Colman and Frederick & R.C. Hall. Sold again at Sotheby's 15 December 1958, lot 281 to R.J. Saunders. Sold again at Sotheby's on 26 Octobery 1959 to Philip and Lionel Robinson. Sold at Sotheby's by the Robinson trust as part of the Bibliotheca Phillippica N.S. 13th part, 25 November 1974, lot 3166 for 12,000 GBP to John Fleming. Sotheby's (1974) notes that "Possibly no. CV (present owner untraced) in Lee's census." Deaccessioned by Meisei University in December 2014, formerly Meisei 2 or MR 0377, "sold for the maintenance of the Library" (West).

Anthony James West, "Update of the Shakespeare First Folio Census," The Library, 7th ser., 24 (2023): 454–64.

The earliest known owner of this copy was Baron Horace de Landau (1824–1903). De Landau made his fortune managing the Rothschild bank in Turin during the railroad boom of the 1860s. His collection of some 60,000 volumes was housed at his home in Italy, Villa de Landau .... It is telling that the only books in English found in the sale were the four Folios of Shakespeare, as sign of the level of respect Shakespeare was held in, even on the continent.
