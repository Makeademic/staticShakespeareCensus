This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Bound in the late nineteenth century by Rivière and Son, in red goatskin. Sewn on six single raised cords laced through the boards. Front bead, double tiered headband, sewn with light green, red, yellow and beige linen over a rolled paper cores. There is gilt on all three edges. Binder's leaves are plain white flyleaf and pastedown.

Mary Child, a mid-17th century owner, made multiple notations regarding current events. But perhaps her most striking note is the one that accompanies her name, 'Mary Child is the true possesor | of this booke', strong words in an era when women were so often silenced by lack of education.
