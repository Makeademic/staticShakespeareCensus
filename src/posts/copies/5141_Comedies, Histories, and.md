In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy of the Folio has connections to lyricists of both the 18th and 20th centuries. The volume is heavily annotated in A Midsummer Night’s Dream and King Lear by Charles Jennens (1700-1773). Jennens was a librettist who worked with Handel on Saul, Israel in Egypt, L’Allegro, il Penseroso ed il Moderato, Messiah, and Belshazzar. The other musically inclined individual who owned this copy, and is perhaps the only African-American private owner of a Folio, was Paul Francis Webster (1907-1984). Webster was an Academy and Grammy Award winning lyricist who attended Cornell and then NYU, though never graduated. Some of his most famous works were “Love is a Many-Splendored Thing,” “The Shadow of Your Smile,” “Tender is the Night,” and the theme song of “Spider-Man.” He was inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 1972.
