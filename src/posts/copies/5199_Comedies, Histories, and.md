This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The binding is full, white pigskin. There are six panels on the spine. 'SHAKESPEARE' is gold- tooled in the second, 'COMEDIES, TRAGEDIES, AND HISTORIES' in the fourth, and 'LONDON 1623' in the sixth. The edges are gilt.  The box in which the volume is preserved also contains the two calfskin-covered binding boards that were removed in 1982 during rebinding. An early owner of this copy was Richard Wright, M.D. (c 1742-1786). Wright attended Emmanuel College, Cambridge and received his A.B. 1762, A.M. 1765, and M.D. 1773. He was made a fellow of the College of Physicians in 1775 and was also a fellow of the Royal Society .... In addition to his medical pursuits, Wright was also a collector of rare books relating to drama and romance.
