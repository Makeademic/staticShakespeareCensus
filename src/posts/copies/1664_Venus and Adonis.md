The library of the great Robert Harley, Earl of Oxford, which the bookseller Osborne dispersed in the middle years of the eighteenth century, contained very few early editions of Shakespeare's works, but the Catalogus Bibliothecae Harleianae (1743-5)) enumerates\r\namong them a copy of Venus and Adonis dated 1602.

Part of a collection of title-pages and fragments of printed books assembled by John Bagford (1650-1716), acquired by Robert Harley (1661-1724) in 1716. Harley's collection became one of the foundation collections of the British Museum in 1753.
