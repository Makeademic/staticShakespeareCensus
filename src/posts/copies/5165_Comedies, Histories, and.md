This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy of the Folio is in excellent condition and a prime example of a trend in rare book commerce in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. As the British empire declined and many aristocratic families lost much of their wealth, the libraries of many stately homes were sold off to Americans. This was the case with the Dryden family’s library, which was sold by Sir Arthur Dryden, 6th Baronet Dryden of Canons Ashby, in 1913, following his father’s death in 1912. The volume wound up in the possession of a succession of book dealers and private owners in the United States, where it has remained ever since. The current owner, Paul G. Allen, who co-founded Microsoft with Bill Gates, generously lends this volume to the Oregon Shakespeare Festival in Ashland, Oregon, for several months each year. Allen’s copy is the only privately held First Folio that is regularly exhibited to the public.
