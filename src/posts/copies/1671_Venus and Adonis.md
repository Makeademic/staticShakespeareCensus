The British Museum copy, which measures 4 5/16 x 3 1/8") is bound in russia, and is badly stained and soiled, with a few leaves mended. It belonged to George Hibbert, of Portland Place, London, at whose sale in 1829 it fetched £1 14s. 0d. This copy is possibly identical with that which was sold bound up in a volume with the Rape of Lucrece (1616) and other poetical tracts, at the sale of Thomas Pearson in 1788, when the whole volume fetched £1 2s. 0d.
