The copies listed here cannot be identified with any one specific issue of the 1632 folio edition.

From Bloomsbury catalogue: "A1 ('To the Reader'), A2 (title with portrait) and A5 (commendatory verses) plus 8 ff. of main text (c2 and last 7 ff.) all replaced in modern facsimile, 7 further preliminary ff. supplied from another, slightly shorter copy, A1 (first f. of The Tempest) mounted & silked, 4 other ff. silked, a few ff. with later repair to fore-corners, some minor marking and browning, with very occasional 18th century marginalia ...  6 later ms. ff. bound in supplying additional section of Cymbeline ... The facsimile leaves are supplied from the 1909 Methuen edition, sympathetically toned to fit." From Sotheby's catalogue: "Tipped-in at the beginning is a typed note recording that this copy was rebound by Edward Lambert in the Bookbinding Department of Leeds Public Libraries in April 1959 ("...The previous binding was in brown half leather...some crude repair work on a few of the first and last leaves was removed and new repair using silk lisse substituted..."). Most of the missing leaves from this copy are supplied in photofacsimile from the copy in the Sterling Library in the University of London Library, bound in brown cloth in a separate volume included with this lot."
