This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Bound in 1954 at the Folger Library by Robert Lunow, in burgundy Russell’s 'Oasis Niger' goatskin. Sewn on 5 sawn-in bands. Reinforced bands were laced through the boards. Flat front bead headbands sewn over vellum and leather cores with blue, beige and white silk. There is gilt on all three edges. Endpapers are marbled paper in French curl on Turkish pattern in burgundy, blue and gray/green colors.

The bookplate found at the front of this volume dates to c 1740 and therefore most likely belonged to Wadham Wyndham (d. 1736). After Wyndham’s death it is not known what happened to the Folio. As some point it was acquired by Francis Fortescue Urquhart (1868–1934). Urquhart sold the Folio through Sotheby’s 598, 26 March 1923. At the sale, the volume was purchased by Quaritch for £260. Quaritch then turned around and sold it to Henry Folger for $1,275 that same month.

The bookplate of Wadham Wyndam (d. 1736) found in this volume is an example of a changing trend in bookplates in England in the 18th century. The bookplate is essentially a copy from an earlier bookplate by Gravelot. This period saw a shift from bookplates that strictly bore a coat of arms, perhaps a motto, and the name of the owner, to bookplates bearing a coat of arms and name integrated into a more ornate image
