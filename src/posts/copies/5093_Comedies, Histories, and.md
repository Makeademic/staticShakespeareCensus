This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The facsimile pages in this copy bear the watermark SHAKESPEARE. This watermark is also seen in the facsimile pages of several other Folios. The natural supposition is that this paper was specially made to serve as facsimile and binding papers for First Folios. Considering the booming business that existed, particularly in the 19th century, for perfecting copies of the Folio it makes perfect sense that there would have been a market for paper made to a standard that would be suitable for the Folio.
