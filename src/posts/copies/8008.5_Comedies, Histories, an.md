On the title page, in pencil: ‘This Copy belonged to Mr. Steevens’.  A small loose note within the Bute F4 records that it is a copy from the library of George Steevens. The Bute F4 may have been Lot 1316 at the sale of his library on 13 May 1800. Machell Stace acted as an agent for John Stuart, 1st Marquess of Bute, and Stace acquired several books for his library - it is likely to have joined the Bute Collection through the 1st Marquess while he continued to build the Bute Collection of Early English Plays. This copy was part of John Crichton-Stuart, 4th Marquess of Bute’s Library at 39 Belgrave Square, London in the 1930s.
