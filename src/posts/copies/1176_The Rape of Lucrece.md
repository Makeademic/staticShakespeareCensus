The copy in the Barton collection at the Boston Public Library has the frontispiece inlaid. This copy was thus described by the bookseller, Thomas Rodd, on October 5, 1835 :-- 'The title-page torn and laid down. The frontispiece inlaid. Several leaves cut into the side margin & dirty. The back margin sewed in. Rodd thought it might be identical with the copy sold in 1827 at the Field sale for £3 19s. 0d. It was purchased by T. P. Barton of New York, from Rodd, in 1835, and bequeathed by Barton \r\nto the Boston Public Library in 1876. It is bound in green morocco by Mackenzie, and the binder has misplaced pages 5 and 8.

The armorial bookplate of the Barton Library is present on the front pastedown.
