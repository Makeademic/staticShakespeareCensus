In this issue, a new bifolium A1.2 was added, including a resetting of the verses "To the Reader" in smaller type on A1v, to make room for the engraved portrait which was moved to that page, and a title page dated 1664 on A2r. The original A1 and A2, including the title page dated 1663, were apparently intended to be removed. In some copies the original was not removed and both title pages and "To the Reader" leaves, or some combination thereof, appear at some point in the volume, as noted under particular copies. This issue also added seven additional plays, which are listed on the 1664 title page. Copies with any of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: 1663 title page, without the portrait, and the additional plays. Title page is patched (inconspicuously), affecting the first line of the title and the end of both lines of the imprint; these areas are touched up and completed in pen facsimile. Wants leaf πA1 (the associated "To the Reader" leaf); a facsim. of another "To the Reader" leaf is supplied. Leaves πb1,2,5,6 are patched (inconspicuously), with text on πb1 completed in pen facsimile and text on πb2,4 touched up with pen and ink. Leaves πb4,6 are closely trimmed at the outer edge, cropping text on πb4 recto and shaving it on πb4 verso and 6 verso. On many pages the head rule is shaved and on some the head rule is cropped and the headline shaved.  On some pages the tail rule is shaved. Leaf I6 is patched (inconspicuously).  Leaf 2O4 went through the press with its upper outer corner turned over the verso; text on this overlapping portion, now affixed, is affected slightly.  On leaf [par.]C3, cropping affects the tail rule (recto and verso), a catchword (recto), and text slightly (verso).  Leaf [par.]G6 is patched, with text supplied in manuscript facsimile, recto and verso. Other imperfections affect text very slightly on 2L4 and 3D5. Variant states and printings, as recorded by James G. McManaway, "New discoveries in the Third Folio," PBSA, v. 70, (1976), 469-80, and other such variations, are identified in file.
