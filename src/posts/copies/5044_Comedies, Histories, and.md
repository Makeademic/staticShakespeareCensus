This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Bound in the late eighteenth century by Roger Payne in red willow-grained (parallel-grained) calfskin. Characteristic of Payne, the headbands have a single front bead on a flat parchment and leather core, sewn with white and silver thread; the thread is made from silver foil (now tarnished) wrapped around a silk core. All three edges are gilt with gauffered decoration (including a floriated crescent arch tool found on the binding of copy 69 [Folger 11]).

This copy was first owned by Colonel John Lane (1609-1667) who sheltered King Charles II in his country house, Bentley Hall, during his flight after the Battle of Worcester in 1651...This copy provides one example among many of how central Bernard Quaritch was in the sale of First Folios. Quaritch sold the volume to Colonel Hargreaves for £2,850 in 1907. At the sale of Colonel Hargreaves’ property, Quaritch then repurchased the Folio for £2,000 (£850 less than he sold it for a few years earlier). Quaritch then sold the copy to Folger.
