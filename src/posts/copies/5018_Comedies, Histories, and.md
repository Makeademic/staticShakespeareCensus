This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The volume was purchased at the N. P. Simes sale, Sotheby’s, 9 July 1886, for £71 by James Rimmell, London; the three other Folios were in the same sale. The bookseller Gilbert F. Ellis bought the volume from Rimmell for £100. Ellis then wrote to Samuel Sandars, 17 July 1886, ‘[I] can promise you to make a very respectable volume of it, if you will let me take the matter in hand’.  Sandars purchased it for an unknown price. Sandars (1837–94) was a Trinity College alumnus, lawyer, book-collector, and founder of the Sandars Readership in Bibliography.
