This excellent copy is exhibited in the British Library’s Ritblat Gallery; with the Stratford First Folio, it is among the most widely seen First Folios in the world. It was purchased in 1818 by Thomas Grenville, a long-time Member of Parliament and First Lord of the Admiralty, who was the foremost English collector of rare books in the first half of the nineteenth century. Like most collectors of his generation, Grenville rebound nearly every book he bought in sumptuous full goatskin stamped with his coat of arms. On his death in 1846, he left his library of over 20,000 volumes to the British Museum. \r\n\r\nPurchased by James Midgeley, Jr. at the 23 April 1792 Sotheby’s sale of the Bibliotheca Elegantissima Monroiana owned by Dr Monro, physician to Bethlehem Hospital. Then, according to Winsor, it was purchased in 1818 at an R. Saunders sale for the record price of 116 guineas by Payne, the bookseller, for the Right Hon. Thomas Grenville. On the binder’s leaf before πA1, Grenville wrote, ‘This . . . is an original and perfect copy, and was purchased by me in its first binding, and in its original state. T.G.’ He left the Folio to the British Museum upon his death in 1846.
