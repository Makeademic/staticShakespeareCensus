This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Several plays have MS lists of actors linked to characters; for instance The Winter’s Tale (Cc2) has MS pen right of printed characters with dashed lines connecting MS names with printed characters: "Garrick" linked to Leontes, "Davies" linked to Camillo, "Pritchard" linked to Hermione, "Miss Pritchard | Bettany" linked to Perdita, "Harvard" linked to Polixenes, "Holland Barry" linked to Florizell, "Berry" linked to Old Shepheard, "Woodward" linked to Clown, "Yates Shuter" linked to Autolicus.

Medium- to dark-brown morocco with a double gilt fillet border near the outer edges of the front and back boards. There is an intricate diamond gilt design stamped into the centre of both boards. There are five bands on the spine with gilt lines on each. The first, third, fourth, fifth, and sixth panels have gilt floral designs. The first panel reads ‘shakespeare | workes’. The edges are untrimmed. The present binding was done in 1978 by Bernard Middleton, OBE, of Clapham.

Perhaps one of the most interesting aspects of this copy is the manner in which the present owner has gone about restoring damaged leaves. At various points between A1 and M3 the bottom outer corners have been repaired by replacing the lost text with the same portion of text taken from loose original First Folio leaves that John Wolfson acquired from Quaritch .... Mary [Cowden Clarke] is one of many examples of an editor of Shakespeare owning a First Folio. Her editions of Shakespeare were published between 1860 and 1897, and although it cannot be confirmed, it is likely she used this copy in her editing work.
