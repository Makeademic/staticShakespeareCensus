Three distinguished eighteenth-century Shakespeare editors at one time owned this copy: Lewis Theobald (1688-1744), Dr. Samuel Johnson (1709-1784),   and George Steevens (1736-1800). Steevens acquired the volume from the publisher Jacob Tonson, who owned the copyright to Shakespeare's works throughout the eighteenth century. The British Library acquired the copy in 1818, as part of the library of Dr. Charles Burney (1757-1817), son of Charles Burney the famed music historian. Burney was dismissed from Cambridge for the offence of borrowing volumes from the library at Cambridge, removing the bookplates and inserting his own, then selling the books in London to fund his extravagant lifestyle. \r\n\r\nThis copy has two title pages, neither is original. The first is a photographic facsimile, the second a drawing by George Steevens (the verso contains his signature).
