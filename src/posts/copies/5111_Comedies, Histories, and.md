This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Folger 80 is a set of sewn-together leaves stored in twenty separate folders made of thick board covered with burgundy wove cloth. Each folder has 'SHAKESPEARE | [NAME OF PLAY(S)] | 1623' gold-tooled on the front cover. The plays in each folder are sewn together in sets of one, two, and three plays, as if ready for binding (except in folder 10). The leaves in folder 10 are not sewn all together, but rather gathered in quires (some of which are still barely attached to each other by sewing).

This copy consists of 395 original leaves that appear to all come from the same copy. The copy is made up of 20 sewn together sections that are housed in separate folders. There are a total of 31 of the original 36 plays and the missing sections were probably taken to sell as individual plays or in order to perfect other copies of the Folio. This is the most likely explanation for why five complete plays were removed from the volume, otherwise it would be expected to see individual leaves missing at other points in the volume. The precision with which the pages were removed certainly suggests the intentional removal of plays rather than loss or damage over time.
