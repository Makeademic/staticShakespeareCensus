This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Toovey changed the binding when he acquired the volume in about 1887. An old binding with a bishop’s armorial bearings was changed for the current one, with the Sydney arms on the front and back. There are double gold-tooled fillets forming borders on the front and back boards. The current binding is early brown calf. The binding has three sets of two holes that were once used to hold ribbon ties. Marks from where the binding used to be chained appear on the front cover.

This copy was brought to America by John Pierpont "J.P." Morgan (1837–1913), the tycoon who founded the financial institutions known collectively as "The House of Morgan" and had major financial interests in a huge number of American railroads of the period .... An interesting physical feature of this copy is the presence of both the cancel leaf from the end of Romeo and Juliet and the leaf containing the prologue to Troilus and Cressida .... Sidney Lee wrote: ‘Said to be originally c 1623 in library of Robert Sidney, second Earl of Leicester (1595-1677). The arms of this nobleman are stamped on cover. Acquired c 1860 by Leonard Laurie Hartley, and sold at sale of his library, May 3, 1886, to James Toovey, the London bookseller, who carefully restored it. It was purchased with James Toovey’s private library by the present owner in 1899.’ Lee discovered at a later date that the current binding on the volume was not original, it had been taken from another volume, and substituted for another therefore the bookplate of Sir William Brownlow of Belton in the County of Lincoln, Baronet, 1698 that is found in the volume belongs to another volume not this Folio.
