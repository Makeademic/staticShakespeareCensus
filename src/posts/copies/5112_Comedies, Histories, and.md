This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Folger 81 and 82 are described together because they were made up from the same original copies. The better copy of each play or group of plays is bound in red as Folger 81; the lesser copy is bound in green as Folger 82. All plays in Folger 82 are also in Folger 81; Missing Leaves in each have been replaced by facsimile leaves. Folger 81 contains two remarkable features. On the leaves of Richard II are traces of a page in André Favyn’s The Theater of Honor and Knighthood (London, 1623), which the Jaggards were printing at the same time as the First Folio. Words from page 414 of Favyn’s text can be clearly read on the Richard II leaves. The other remarkable feature of both 81 and 82 are several leaves from Hamlet marked up as prompt copies for performance.
