There is only one known owner of this copy before it came to the State Library of New South Wales: Sir Richard Tangye—a Cornish engineer and machine tool manufacturer. Along with his partner and younger brother, George, Tangye grew his Birmingham manufacturing business, opening branches on the continent and in Australia, and gaining fame when in 1878 their hydraulic jacks were used to position the enormous Cleopatra's Needle on the Thames Embankment .... On 13 February 1885, Richard and George donated the copy to the Public Library of New South Wales—renamed the State Library of New South Wales in 1975—in Sydney: a city that was home to a Tangyes Limited branch.
