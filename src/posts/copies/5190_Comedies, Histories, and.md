This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Title page and Ben Jonson's "To the reader" are both wanting and supplied in facsimile. The portrait by Martin Droeshout on the t.p. is inlaid and genuine. Several leaves have been torn and repaired. Pagination of the 26 unnumbered pages of Troilus and Cressida has been supplied in ink, 91-115. Title of King John has been written in ms. in 17th-cent. hand on blank leaf facing the play. Pen trials at foot of leaf k1; "Exeunt" written in ms. on leaf a38v; various errors (pagination and others) corrected in ms.
