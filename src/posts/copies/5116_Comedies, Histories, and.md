This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Bound size: 322 x 203 mm. The binding is dark brown goatskin that is slightly scuffed on the edges, binding, and back cover. There are three blind fillets around all the edges that meet in the corners to form a checkerboard pattern. Blind fillets also occur on the inside edges of the binding on both the front and back covers. The spine has five ribs with three blind fillets above and below each of them. A gold-tooled coat of arms of the Guildhall appears above the first rib.

Zaehnsdorf, 1918.
