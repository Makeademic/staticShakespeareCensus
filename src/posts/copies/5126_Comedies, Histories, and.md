This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Imperfect: wanting the two unsigned preliminary leaves, with verses by Digges and I.M., and names of the actors. The preliminary verses "To the reader" are inlaid on a leaf which is red-ruled on recto and verso; other preliminary leaves are hinged. Inserted after binder's leaves at front: single sheet, printed on verso only, being facsimiles of Shakespeare's signature from various documents; source unknown. (Another copy at this location, Rasmussen & West # 218, has been missing since 1972, when it was stolen.)

Acquired ca. 1744 by Martin Folkes, F.R.S. (1690-1754). Sold at Folkes's sale Feb 21, 1756, by Samuel Baker, for £3 3s. to Dr. John Monro (1715-1791), an associate of George Steevens (1736-1800). It seems likely that Monro left this copy to Steevens, from whom it subsequently passed to the second Earl Spencer, probably ca. 1790. It was certainly in Earl Spencer's library before 1822, when it was catalogued by T.F. Dibdin, Earl Spencer's librarian at Althrop.
