This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be fold at his shop at the signe of the Blacke Beare in Pauls Church-yard" [DEEP]

Extensive marginal annotations by John Prater, who owned the book at least from 1698-1709, chiefly stage directions and descriptions of the plates in Rowe's edition of 1709, as well as a biography of Shakespeare condensed from Rowe, a ten-line continuation of Hugh Holland's commendatory poem (dated 1698), with the attribution to Holland scratched out; the names of the 7 apocryphal plays added in F3 are listed on the Catalogue leaf. Bound in the volume are: several leaves of manuscript notes by Malone on Romeo and Juliet, Troilus and Cressida, and Much Ado About Nothing are bound in at the front of the volume; drawing of Shakespeare’s monument in Holy Trinity Church, signed J Jordan; notes by Mary Bradford Whiting, dated January 1889, concerning “The M.S. notes in the copy of the 2nd. Folio Edition of Shakespeare’s plays in the Bodleian Library” (i.e. this copy), identifying much of the marginalia as taken from Nicholas Rowe’s edition. Price in manuscript on leaf before frontispiece poem "£3.0.0". Quotation from Tertullian on front endpaper: “Verum quodcunque PRIMUM, adulterum quodcunque POSTERIUS”.
