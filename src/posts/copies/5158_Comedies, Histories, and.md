This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Samuel J. Tilden (1814-1886), the owner of this copy who bequeathed it to the New York Public Library, was a prominent attorney and politician. His career was defined by his involvement in bringing down the notorious William ‘Boss’ Tweed and his Tammany Hall power base, as well as his involvement in the disputed presidential election of 1876. Tilden was the Democratic candidate for president in 1876 and won the popular vote over his opposition Rutherford B. Hayes. However, there was a problem in the Electoral College and Tilden ultimately lost the election. From that point onward his political career was essentially over. The rest of his life was devoted to philanthropic work in New York and his greatest legacy was the New York Public Library, which was established from a bequest left in his will.
