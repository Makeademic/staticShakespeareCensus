This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The volume is housed in a brown conservator’s case.  The binding is brown. There are five raised hubs on the spine. “SHAKESPEARE” is gold-tooled in the second panel and “FIRST FOLIO | 1623” is in the third. The other panels have gold-tooled floral designs and other blind-stamped decorations. The front and back boards have double gold-fillet borders.

Apprently the autographs of ‘J. Gurney’ and ‘J. Harington’ appeared in this copy at one time. Lee’s entry states this, however, the autographs can no longer be found. It seems likely that these manuscript notations appeared on either pre-original or post-original binder’s leaves that were lost when the volume was rebound. A physical quirk of the volume is the damage to the corners of leaves C1-G5. The damage occurs in varying forms with some leaves feathered, others have been heavily folded, and others were so badly damaged they were repaired at some point.
