This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Many notes in the Histories match historic names with characters. The Comedies and, to a lesser extent, the Histories are annotated, in a late 17th-century hand, with lists of dramatis personae and identifications of scene locations. The MS note on ll6v of Macbeth is a misquotation of this excerpt from John Dryden’s 'Essay of Dramatic Poesy': 'But Shakespeare’s magic could not copied be; | Within that circle none durst walk but he.'

There is strong evidence that this copy was owned by Col. John Hutchinson (1615-1664), a man famous for being one of the signers of the death warrant of King Charles I. Hutchinson ultimately died a prisoner of Charles II at Sandown Castle. His life was recorded by his wife, Lucy Hutchinson (1620-1681), and published by his ancestor, Rev. Julius Hutchinson (1750-1811), in 1806. After Col. Hutchinson’s death, the volume passed to his half-brother Charles Hutchinson, (c 1631-?) and then down the generations of the Hutchinson family until it was finally sold at auction in 1913.
