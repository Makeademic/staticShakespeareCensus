This issue lists H. Herringman, E. Brewster, and R. Bentley as publishers in the imprint.

Folger copy 24 wants the "verses" leaf with port. ([pi]1) and leaf [superscript pi]A2. The other prelim. leaves are mutilated--the title leaf ([pi]2) cut down to the rules of the title page and mounted, with mutilation of the lower outer corner affecting rules; [superscript pi]A1 with mutilation of the outer margin and tail affecting the text very slightly; [superscript pi]A3 patched at the outer margin and tail; and [superscript pi]A4 patched, with mutilation of the tail affecting rules.  Leaf [superscript pi]A4 is misbound before [superscript pi]A3. All prelim. leaves have been extended. Leaves 4B6-4C2 are wanting. Some leaves are closely trimmed at head, affecting the top rule and on a few leaves the page number.   Leaf Y1 is mutilated at head, affecting the top rule and the running head, recto and verso. Imperfections affect text very slightly on B1, 2E1, 2P6, 2X1, and 3V6. Mounted or bound-in are:--aut. let. sig., John Adam, 49 Esplanade, Saturday, [18--], to "My dear Sir" (inside front cover).--wood engraving, "Portraits of the Sovereigns, Contemporaries, Biographers [sic no comma] Editors, Commentators, Players, and Illustrators of Shakespeare" signed "Thomas" with, on verso, "Shakespeare’s Works. ..." (bound before front flyleaves).--photoreproduction of the title page of Plutarch, Lives of the Noble Grecians and Romaines Compared, London, 1612 (2d front flyleaf verso).--photograph, presumably of Paton standing next to a table with a book and inkstand, trimmed to the figure, signed, below: "Allan Park Paton"([pi2, title leaf, verso).--fragments of a note in the hand of Paton (rear flyleaf verso). Inserted loose were various scraps, newspaper clippings, etc., and photoreproductions of title pages, of Guevara, Dial of Princes (London, 1582), and of the Plutarch (1612; duplicate of the copy above); now removed to file, with inventory. Paton treats the Plutarch (1612) and The Dial (1582) and specifically the copies pictured (supposedly Shakespeare’s own, then in the Greenock Library) in his Introduction to his edition of Cor. (1880; The Hamnet Shakespere, 6), xix-xlvi.

Text annotated by Paton in pursuance of his theory of "emphasis-capitals", especially Merry Wives (C6 recto-E3 verso), John (2B1 recto-2C4 verso), Romeo (*3D3 recto-*3E8 verso), Oth. (3K2 recto-3M3 ector), and Antony (3M3 verso-3O4 recto), each copiously annotated, except John, sparsely.  Cf. Paton, ed., The Hamnet Shakespere (1877-1896). Otherwise, the pastedowns and flyleaves are annotated by Paton and variously, including an inscription (19th cent.) from "Mr. Jardine" to Horatio McCulloch (front pastedown verso); full list on file.
