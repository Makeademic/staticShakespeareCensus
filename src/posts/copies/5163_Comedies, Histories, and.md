This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Includes a proof-sheet in King Lear, one of six known examples of proofs in F1.

The volume is housed in a sturdy blue clamshell case. The binding is a red goatskin Bedford binding. It is ornamented with gold-tooled framing and characteristic Bedford ornamentation (acorns) on the front and back boards and on the fore-edges and turn-ins. The binding must have been done after Jolley acquired it in 1823 because the sale catalogue of Garrick’s library describes the former binding as half calf.
