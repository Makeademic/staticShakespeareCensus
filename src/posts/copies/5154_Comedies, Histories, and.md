This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The life of James Lenox (1800-1880), the only known private owner of this copy...[was] devoted to philanthropic pursuits. He also took great pleasure in collecting rare books. Some of the highlights of his collection were two copies of the First Folio, copies 165 and 167, and a Gutenberg Bible, which was the first to arrive in America in 1847. He built a library to house his collection that was open to the public. Upon his death the library was bequeathed to the city of New York and was eventually integrated into the New York Public Library.
