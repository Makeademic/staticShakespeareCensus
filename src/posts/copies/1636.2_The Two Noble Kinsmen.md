Offered for sale by Sokol Books, £125,000. Sold by Bonhams, 24 March 2015, £47,500, to Sokol Books. Auctioned as part of the Library of Hugh Selbourne, M.D. From the Bonhams catalogue: "Provenance: 'Halliwell's Sale. May 1856. Lot 331. William Tite', note on front endpaper, with note in another hand "This binding cost me £1.18.0"; presumably the copy offered in the Tite sale in 1874 (lot 2762, sold to Hazlitt), also in red morocco by Bedford." From Sokol Books catalogue: "This copy is presumably the one offered in the Tite sale in 1874, also in red morocco by Bedford., lot 2762, which was sold to Hazlitt. William Carew Hazlitt (1834-1913) was a bibliographer and Shakespeare collector, grandson of the essayist William Hazlitt .... The collection, itself of great literary importance, was sold in New York, 1918."
