This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The Second Folio title leaf bound at end of the volume bears the autograph ‘Anne Lady Crewe’ which likely refers to Lady Anne Crewe [Anne Armine] (d. 1719). This leaf of the volume also bears the stamp ‘G. Steevens’. It is unclear how the volume came into the possession of George Steevens (1736–1800), the Shakespeare scholar and editor, or how long it remained in his ownership. It is uncertain whether Crewe or Steevens should be considered owners of this volume since the markings that relate to them are found on an F2 leaf that has been inserted. It is possible that Crewe and Steevens were both owners of the same Second Folio from which the leaf was taken.
