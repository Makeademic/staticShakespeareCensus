This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy bears manuscript annotations relating to the running of an estate and the sale or purchase of oxen .... Another interesting aspect of this volume is the number of pages that seem to retain the original deckled edges of the paper. This means that many pages of the volume are uneven and have never been trimmed following the manufacturing of the paper. The wide margins on the paper also support the notion that the pages are untrimmed. This lends credence to the theory that the volume is contained in its original binding and that this original binder chose to leave the pages exactly as they were when they arrived in his shop.
