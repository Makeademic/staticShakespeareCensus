This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy of the Folio was given to Yale by Norman Holmes Pearson (1909-1975). Pearson graduated from Yale in 1932 and later taught at the University in the English and American Studies departments. He was perhaps most famous for his time with the Office of Strategic Services in London during World War II. For his distinguished service he received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in the United States, the Médaille de la Reconnaissance française, and the Ordre national de la Légion d'honneur in France, and Order of St. Olav, Knight's Cross, 1st class, in Norway.
