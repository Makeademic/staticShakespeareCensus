These copies form a group that is sometimes referred to as F5, but they are better described as later states of F4. Presumably because the printer of the middle section of F4 (from King John through Romeo and Juliet) failed to provide the publishers with the correct number of sheets, in 1700 seventeen sheets (68 pages) were reprinted for inclusion alongside the remaining sheets. These newly printed sheets lack the boxed-rule lines on the sides and foot of the page.

This copy includes the title page with three publishers in the imprint (Wing S2915). Leaves F4, X1, 2G6, 2N6, 3V2,3,4, and 3Y6 supplied from another copy, probably also Q3, 2G3.4, 2T3.4, and *3D1.6, and perhaps also *3B1. Contains reprinted sheets 2B3.4, 2I2.5, 2I3.4, 2M1.6, 2N1.6 (except wanting reprinted 2N6), 2N2.5, 2O3.4, 2X2.5, 2X3.4, 2Y1.6, 2Z2.5, 2Z3.4, *3B2.5, *3C2.5, and *3C3.4. Imperfections affect text on F4 and 2L2, slightly on *3B4, and very slightly on A4, E1, K2, 2E1, 2X6, 3R6, and 3V4.
