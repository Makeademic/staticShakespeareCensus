Lehigh University, which has owned this Folio since 1887, was originally founded in 1865 as a technical school. However, unlike other technical institutions of that era, Lehigh was a true university, providing students with a grounding in both the sciences and classics. As part of this larger vision Lehigh’s library was relentlessly expanded for a number of years. Another interesting aspect of Lehigh is that Asa Packer (1805-1879), the founder of the Lehigh, provided such a large endowment that, for a time, the students attended free of charge after sitting a competitive entrance exam.
