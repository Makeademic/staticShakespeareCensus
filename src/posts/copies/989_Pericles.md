The interesting copy in the Barton collection in the Boston Public Library belonged to George Steevens, whose autograph it bears. At Steevens' sale in 1800 it was bought for the Duke of Roxburghe's collection for £1 15s. 0d., and was acquired by Thomas Jolley, F.S.A., whose autograph and book-plate are both inserted in it. At Jolley's sale in 1844 it passed through the bookseller, Thomas Rodd, for £13 to the American collector, T. P. Barton, whose books were presented to the Boston Public Library in 1870. The copy, which is slightly foxed, is half-bound in old red morocco.

Armorial bookplate of Thomas Jolley, with Jolley's autograph on front free endpaper. Autograph of George Steevens on title page. Armorial ink stamp of John Ker, 3rd Duke of Roxburghe, on title page verso. Armorial bookplate of the Barton Library on rear pastedown.
