The Eton First Folio is unusual for having bound-in about a hundred engraved portraits of figures from the plays. The portraits originated in Edmond Malone's 1790 edition. This addition—almost certainly the largest to a First Folio anywhere in the world—was made by Anthony Morris Storer, an Old Etonian (1760-1764) and important 18th-century book collector. He bequeathed his collection, including the First Folio and copies of the Second and Third Folios, to Eton College. The Fourth Folio owned by Eton came from the Stowe collection in 1896.
