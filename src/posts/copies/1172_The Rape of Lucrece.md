A defective copy (consisting of twenty-seven leaves of the thirty) is in the Edinburgh University Library. The measurements are 5 1/8 x 3 5/16". It has no title-page, and the leaves C and C2 (lines 764-903) are missing. The bottom edges are closely shaved throughout. It was bound by Tuckett. It was presented, in 1872, to the Edinburgh University by J. O. Halliwell-Phillipps, who, in a manuscript note, describes in as a unique exemplar, in ignorance of the survival of any other copy of the 1632 edition. Halliwell-Phillipps had, in his Folio Shakespeare (1865), dated this defective copy before 1616, assigning it tentatively to the year 1610, but his final opinion that it was issued in 1632 is undoubtedly right.
