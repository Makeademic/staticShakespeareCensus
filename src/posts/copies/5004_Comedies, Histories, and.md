This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

28 pages incomplete as follows: the first 12 pages, Tempest, p. 3-4, Tempest + Gent. p. 19-38, Merry Wives p. 47-48, Troilus p. 97-100, Coriolanus p. 25-26, Titus p. 33-34, 37-38, 47-48, Macbeth: p. 143-144, Hamlet: p. (1) 257- (1) 258, Cymbeline p. 389- (3) 993).

It is almost certain that this volume comes from the collection of the English Jesuit college of Saint-Omer. The name "Nevill" has been written in ink on the current first page, possibly the pseudonym of Basil Thomas Scarisbrick, then Eccleston (1713? -1789), educated in Saint-Omer around 1727, and who gave a series of works to his old school in 1736. Every 100 pages approximately, the initials "PS" were printed using an awl. Identified in 2014 by librarian Rémy Cordonnier in the public library at Saint-Omer, having been assumed previously—in part due to missing preliminary leaves—to be an 18th-century edition (SW).
