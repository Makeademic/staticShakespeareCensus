These copies form a group that is sometimes referred to as F5, but they are better described as later states of F4. Presumably because the printer of the middle section of F4 (from King John through Romeo and Juliet) failed to provide the publishers with the correct number of sheets, in 1700 seventeen sheets (68 pages) were reprinted for inclusion alongside the remaining sheets. These newly printed sheets lack the boxed-rule lines on the sides and foot of the page.

This copy lacks the title page, so it cannot be determined which issue of F4 it originally represented. It contains only one reprinted leaf (2O4) of the so-called F5 sheets, and this one leaf is supplied from another copy. Lacks all before A2. Also wanting are T3.4, U3.4, 2F1, 2G3.4, *3D3.4, *3E8, 3D3.4, 3H3, 3O5-3Q6 (Cymb. entire), 3V1, and 4A1-end. Many leaves are soiled, many have edges torn or feathered, many are water-stained, and many are wormed. Worming Q6-Y2 at the head, affects text slightly or very slightly passim. Worming Y3-2P4 (except 2N1 and 2O4, supplied from another copy) at the tail, affects text or slightly or very slightly affects text on most pages. Other imperfections affect text on A2v, S1, 2K4,5, 2N1, 2O4, 2U3-5, 3A3,4, and 3K5, slightly on 2F5 and 2K3,6, and very slightly on 17 other leaves (list on file). Contains reprinted leaf 2O4.  Supplied from another copy are: 2N1 and 2O4, both unwormed untypically and 2O4 disjunct and unexpectedly reprinted. Inserted: "London; Engraved for Harrison’s Edition of Rapin[.]  William Shakespear.  [image, after the Chandos portrait]  Goldar sculpt[.]  In the Possession of John Nicoll Esqr.  Published as the Act directs Septr. 30, 1785."  From Rapin de Thoyras, History of England, London, 1785-89. This copy--cannibalized as a stock of leaves, with many of the remaining leaves showing lines of paste as if used as a surface on which another leaf being brushed with paste was placed.
