The copies listed here cannot be identified with any one specific issue of the 1685 folio edition.

Folger copy 5 wants the "verses" leaf with port. ([pi]1) and the title leaf ([pi]2) and all else through C5. Wants 4A3 to end. Leaves 2U1.6, uniquely wormed, are supplied from another copy. Imperfections affect text on *3C2, *3E4, and 4A2, slightly on 2G1 and 2O5, and very slightly on 13 other leaves (list on file). Mounted or bound-in are:-- ephemera relating to this copy (inside front cover).--ms. notes by Paton (on front pastedown and on front flyleaves or on leaves tipped thereonto).--photoreproduction of the title page of Plutarch, Lives of the Noble Grecians and Romaines Compared, London, 1612 (front flyleaf verso), annotated by Paton (on the flyleaf verso below the photoreproduction). Paton treats the Plutarch (1612), specifically the copy the title page of which is pictured in the photoreproduction (supposedly Shakespeare’s own, then in the Greenock Library) in his Introduction to his edition of Cor. (1880; The Hamnet Shakespere, 6), xix-xlvi. Inserted loose, mounted, or bound-in were:--ephemera relating to the Shakespeare tercentenary of 1864.--ms. notes by and ephemera relating to Allan Park Paton.--various clippings of text and illustrations, including "At Hamnet’s Funeral," by the Rev. W. H. Hutton, and a review of a performance in Edinburgh of "Mr. Forbes Robertson in the ’Sacrament of Judas’".-- "Merry Wives of Windsor.  Act 1. Scene 1. . . . Painted by R. Smirke.  Engraved by M. Haughton.  Pub. June 4, 1801, by J. & J. Boydell at the Shakespeare Gallery. ...", stipple engraving.--ephemera relating to Isabella Dallas Glyn.  Now housed separately, with inventories, accompanying this copy.

Text annotated and marked in multiple hands, chiefly that of (1) Allan Park Paton (in pencil), especially Romeo (*3D3 recto-*3E6 verso; sparsely annotated) and Antony (3M3 verso-3O4 verso)--partly in pursuance of his theory of "emphasis-capitals" (cf. Paton, ed., The Hamnet Shakespere [1877-1896]), and (2) an earlier annotator (in ink, fine line) who mostly tracks the equivalent paging of the First Folio (1623) with numbers in the inner and outer margins but who annotated Hamlet (3E6 recto-3H1 verso; sparsely) and Lear (3H2 recto-3K1 verso; very sparsely) and who also probably is the annotator who marked Pericles (3R1 recto-3S4 recto; copiously) with underlining in red and with regular numbering (in ink, fine line) of segments of the text.  Other marking includes underlining in red or blue pencil, passim.
