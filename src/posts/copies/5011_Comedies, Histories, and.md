This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This volume was inherited by George IV [from George III], who was an ardent book and art collector throughout his life. George IV retained his own First Folio—still held by the Royal Library at Windsor (West 42)—while presenting this copy to the British Library in 1823, three years after his father’s death. The selection of which copy to donate and which to keep was perhaps unsurprising given the relatively poor condition of this volume. Although the binding appears pristine, the pages therein are riddled with holes resulting in the loss of text on virtually every page. \r\n\r\nAn interesting quirk of this volume is the sketching found in 2 Henry IV. Near the end of the play, on gg7v, is a sketch of a cannon firing at the word 'FINIS' as well as several sketches of a man’s head with the name William Blunt written beneath them. This is yet another example of a reader of the Folio leaving behind a personal touch.
