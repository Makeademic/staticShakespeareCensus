This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Prelim. leaves [1-2] (incl. t.p.) in late 19th-century facsimile. Letter "o" erased on p. 73 (2nd group), column 1, line 8. Pages 139-140 (last group) with remains of original deckle edge. Stain caused by a rosebud(?) on p. 395 (last group).

A likely candidate for the mysterious Lady Drake is Elizabeth Beatrice Fuller Eliot Drake of Buckland Abbey (1862–1937). A descendant of Sir Francis Drake, Fuller Eliot Drake was the daughter of Sir Francis Fuller Eliot Drake, 2nd Baronet. Although her father would have still been alive at the time Quaritch acquired the copy from her, it is possible she was referred to as Lady Drake due to the fact that her mother had died in 1840 and she did not marry until 1887. In his response to Lee’s enquiries about the volume John Boyd Tharcher stated that the volume had always been in Lady Drake’s family. If this is the case it is fascinating to consider the possibility that John Lloyd actually owned the volume after Lady Drake and that the volume was owned in continuous succession by the descendants of Sir Francis Drake.
