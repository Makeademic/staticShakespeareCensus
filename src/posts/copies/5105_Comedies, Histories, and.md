This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy contains the manuscript annotation ‘Elias Sutton Pharmacopola Londinensis.’ It is possible that Sutton was an early owner and that he was the Elias Sutton mentioned as obtaining a marriage license in July 1690. This would make sense since the Sutton documented in 1690 was an apothecary of St. Giles, Cripplegate, London and the text to which he refers in his notation, Pharmacopoeia Londinensis, is a medical text. The marriage records state that he was around 24 at the time and therefore, if this Elias Sutton did write the note, the note would have had to have been written sometime before 1750.
