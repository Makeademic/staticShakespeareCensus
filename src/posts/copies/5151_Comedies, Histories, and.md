This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Currently bound in three volumes, one for each genre.

The Folio was bequeathed to Isaac Reed by John Henderson, and given to Reed by Henderson’s widow on 3rd February 1786, as documented in a MS inscription in the Comedies volume. It was later sold to a ‘JW’ for £38 (Lot 8663) in the sale of Reed’s library in 1807. Correspondence dated from 1807 in the Bute Collection at Mount Stuart notes that John Stuart, 4th Earl and 1st Marquess of Bute (1744-1814), thought the Folio in Reed’s 1807 sale was ‘too dear’. The Bute F1 was owned by John Crichton-Stuart, 3rd Marquess of Bute (1847-1900) and is listed in the Catalogue of the Library at St. John’s Lodge, Regent’s Park, London in 1896. This suggests that the First Folio likely joined the Bute Collection at some point during the 19th century and was acquired by either the 1st, 2nd, or 3rd Marquess of Bute.
