In this issue, a new bifolium A1.2 was added, including a resetting of the verses "To the Reader" in smaller type on A1v, to make room for the engraved portrait which was moved to that page, and a title page dated 1664 on A2r. The original A1 and A2, including the title page dated 1663, were apparently intended to be removed. In some copies the original was not removed and both title pages and "To the Reader" leaves, or some combination thereof, appear at some point in the volume, as noted under particular copies. This issue also added seven additional plays, which are listed on the 1664 title page. Copies with any of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: no title leaf, no "verses" leaf, but with the additional plays. The 1664 title and "verses" leaves are supplied in facsim. Leaves πA3-πb6 are inset; text toward the margins is touched up with pen and ink and completed in manuscript facsim. Leaves B1,6 and B2,5, the watermarks of which redound, belong to different copies.  Most likely B1 (inset) and B2 (inset) were supplied from another copy, but B5,6--as all the leaves B3-C2, which are remargined at the tail--may have been supplied from another copy.  The head of C2 is patched with a piece supplied from another genuine leaf; the headline, recto and verso, is touched up with pen and ink and completed in manuscript facsim. Leaves [par.]A2,5, the watermarks of which redound, belong to different copies. Many leaves passim are discolored by chemical treatment.  Some of these leaves are likely supplied from another copy. Imperfections affect text on a4 and very slightly on E4 and [par.]F1. Variant states and printings, as recorded by James G. McManaway, "New discoveries in the Third Folio," PBSA, v. 70, (1976), 469-80, and other such variations, are identified in file.
