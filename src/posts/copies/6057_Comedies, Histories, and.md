Charles I copy, with his signature ("CR"); presented by the King to Sir Thomas Herbert (1606-82); probably in the collection of Richard Mead (1673-1754); probably purchased at his sale (lot 1744, 7 May 1755) by Anthony Askew (1722-74); purchased at his sale (lot 347, 14 February 1775) by George Steevens (1736-1800); purchased at his sale (lot 1314, 21 May 1800) for the Royal Library during the reign of George III (r. 1760-1820); George Steevens annotated the fly-leaf to note that Thomas Herbert had been Master of the Revels to Charles I. When the book was sold at the sale of Steevens's library in 1800, it was purchased for the Royal Library by George III. Reading the annotation about Herbert, the King noticed that Steevens had erred, so he also annotated the book to record that Herbert had in fact been Charles I's Groom of the Bedchamber.
