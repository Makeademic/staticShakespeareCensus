This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Most of the preliminary leaves are in facsimile, as well as some leaves in Cymbeline; the portrait is original but inlaid in a facsimile title page.

Lee was aware of this copy, and had documents on it (including a completed questionnaire from Campbell Munro, the father of the owner in 1908, Hugh Thomas Munro), but he published nothing on it. Anthony James West was unable to locate it during his initial searches, so it received no number in his Census. However, he described it in as much detail as Campbell Munro’s questionnaire allowed (West, Census, pp. 307–309). Sir Thomas Munro, an early owner, was a member of the East India Company and an important administrator in India. His grandson, Hugh Thomas Munro, was a famous mountaineer who was commissioned by the Scottish Mountaineering Club to publish information regarding summits over 3,000 feet; he eventually produced a detailed list and description of 283 peaks.
