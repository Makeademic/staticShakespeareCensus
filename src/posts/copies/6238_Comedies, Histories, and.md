It is not known when the copy of the Second Folio was acquired, and there is no evidence for Soane's assertion in the 1835 Description of the house and museum that 'Amongst the more rare and valuable works in the bookcases in [the Library Dining Room] are ... the four folio editions of Shakespeare’s works, formerly the property of John Philip Kemble'; only the copy of the First Folio (q.v.), bought at the Boswell sale in 1825, is securely known to have belonged to Kemble.
