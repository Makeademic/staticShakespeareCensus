MS notes on front fly leaf, along with a typescript transcription, by members of the Gill family, British Ambassador Lord Halifax, and Librarian Archibald MacLeish detailing its provenance. Received in 1942 by MacLeish from Halifax on behalf of the Gill family, given “to help the Empire war effort” and “presented to the United States of America as a mark of the gratitude of the ordinary people of England for all they have done and are doing.” MacLeish notes the folio is a “symbol of the common ties of language and particularly of poetry which bind our peoples together at this hour of their common trial—their common struggle for the freedom of men, and above all, the freedom of men’s minds.” Given in April 1882 by Margaret D'Aubney Hanson, widow of Samuel Hanson (1804–1883), to Thomas Hornblower Gill; subsequently inherited by Ernest Compton Gill and Ernest Walter Brudenell Gill. Also inscribed: "Thomas Dove, 1706"; "Cormelian"; "William Love"; and illegible note dated 1787.
