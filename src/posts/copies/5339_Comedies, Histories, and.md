Note from a Folger curator on front flyleaf, dated "17 Sep. 58": "The loose leaf, uncorrected, but without proofreader's marks, clearly came at some time from what is now Folio 79. Was the extraction made before H.C.F. bought 79? By Halliwell-Phillipps?'  Longer note (by Halliwell-Phillipps?) on previous front flyleaf: "This unique printer's proof of a page of the first folio Edition of Shakespeare is of remarkable interest in connexion with the question of the care bestowed upon the printing of that celebrated volume, & hence its relative authority. The corrections are restricted to a single page, the other side of hte leaf corresponding with the ordinary copies; so that the page here shown is probably a revise of a corrected proof, this particular portion having, for some reason, escaped revision with the rest. It was found in a parcel of fragments of the first folio in my possession, but the most anxious search failed in the discovery of any other page similarly treated. The corrections in the second column are the most interesting, especially those which show that the original corrector of the press paid great attention to the punctuation. Thus in line 11, the comma is removed from its position after the word *that* to its proper localis(?) - after the word *horse*; in line 25, a comma improperly introduced is marked for omission; & in line 52, another is correctly inserted after the word *speake*. The corrections in line 17 are curious. The compositor had spelt *rheume* without the letters *he*, which the corrector inserts, but the latter, not wishing to run the line over, takes out two superfluous letters in other words. The careful mode in which this page is corrected will perhaps do something to increase the authority of the first folio. Thus, in the seventh line of the second column, the passage, "at the full of tide", originally printed, "at the of full tide", is carefully altered. Some editors consider that the reading of the second folio, "at full of tide", is correct but it is reasonable to suppose that had the MS. wanted the particle *the*, the same careful hand would have marked it for omission."
