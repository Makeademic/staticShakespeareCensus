This issue lists H. Herringman, E. Brewster, and R. Bentley as publishers in the imprint.

Folger copy 26 wants the "verses" leaf with port. ([pi]1); facsims. supplied, photographic (of the port.) and pen-and-ink (of the signature of the port. and the "verses"). Lower corners of title leaf [pi]2 patched, affecting rules on the tp ([pi]2 recto) very slightly. Lower margin of 4C1 is patched, and 4C2 (mounted) is mutilated, affecting the page number, rules, and "FINIS" on recto, supplied in manuscript facsim. Gathering *3D (short and annotated distinctively), 3B5 (short), and 3G2.5 (short) may be supplied from another copy or copies. Imperfections affect text slightly on 2F1 (also rules), *3B5, and 3V6 and very slightly on 13 other leaves (list on file).

With annotations in manuscript in three distinguishable hands:--At the head of Temp. (A1 recto): "this play is lately revived by Mr Driden and much more correct", in hand A (ca. 1700).  [Dryden’s Temp. was first peformed Nov. 1667; see London Stage, I, 123.]--Running title of Two Gent. on B4 recto corrected from the erroneous "The Tempest." to "Two Gentlemen of Verona", in hand B, that of J. Hanington (see below).--At the head of M. for M. (E4 recto): "for the plot see ...", in hand C (17th-cent.).--At the head of Merch. (M6 verso): "this play is lately revived under the title of the Jew of Venice", in hand A (ca. 1700).  [George Granville’s Jew of Venice first performed Feb. 1701; see London Stage, II, 7.]--At the head of Shrew (Q2 verso): "This Play is Lately Revived and Acted under the title of The Taming of ye Shrew or Sany [sic] the scot ...", in hand C (17th-cent.).  [John Lacy’s Sauny the Scot first performed April 1667; see London Stage, I, 106.]--At the head of Oldcastle (3X1 recto): "John Oldcastle came of worthy parents ...", in hand B, that of J. Hanington (see below).--At the end of Oldcastle (3Y5 recto): "Lord Cobham, being denounced ..."  Signed: "J. Hanington  Sepr 1st 1770", in hand B. Also other marks and annotations in manuscript passim.
