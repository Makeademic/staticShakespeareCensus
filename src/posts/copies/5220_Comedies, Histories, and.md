This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The t.p. and the Ben Jonson verses facing it are in facsimile.

Richard Oswald (1705-1784) was probably the first member of the Oswald family to own this copy of the Folio. Oswald led the British side of negotiations for peace with America in 1782 .... The first American owner of this copy was Darwin P. Kingsley (1857-1932). Kingsley was the president of New York Life Insurance from 1906 to 1930. During Kingsley’s tenure as president, the New York Life building, with its famous gold top, was built in New York.
