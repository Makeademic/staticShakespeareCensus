This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The earliest known owner of this volume was Alexander Bannatyne Stewart (1836-1880) of Glasgow .... This copy also contains a rather puzzling manuscript annotation on bbb6v. The notation reads ‘at ye Returne to allow 5s–6d | Tho: Bourne.’ Peter Blayney observes that Thomas Bourne was a London bookseller in the 1620s and 1630s .... This provocative theory, however, may be undermined by the fact that the manuscript note in question appears on a facsimile page. The pen-and-ink facsimile of bbb6 by John Harris dates from the first half of the 19th century. It is possible, of course, that the old paper stock Harris used to make his facsimile had the Bourne annotation on its verso.
