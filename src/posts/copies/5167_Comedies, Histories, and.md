This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

According to an unsubstantiated tradition, this copy was owned by Cotton Mather (1663-1728), making it perhaps the first copy of the First Folio to arrive in the United States, and that the child of an early owner was caught tearing pages out of the volume (which is indeed missing several of the preliminary pages). Another intriguing aspect of the copy is the series of autographs at the end of the volume, which include the signatures of a number of great 19th-century American actors. On the final binder's leaf, the politician Edward Everett has written: "It is said that the manuscript of the Pandects at Florence used to be exhibited with lighted tapers, as a sacred relic. I should be more inclined to pay that honor to a copy of the First Folio of Shakespeare." Acting greats Adelaide Ristori, Fanny Janauschek, Edwin Booth, Charles Fechter, Charlotte Cushman, Tommaso Salvini, Lawrence Barrett, Ernest Rossi, William Warren, Helena Modjeska, Edward S. Willard, and Constant Coquelin all signed the volume.
