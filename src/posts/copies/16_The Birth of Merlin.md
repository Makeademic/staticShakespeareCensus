Spine of "Old Plays, Vol. 59" (Mal. 216) pasted onto the front pastedown.  Front flyleaf with contents list for Mal 216.  Note by Malone on front flyleaf: "The only edition. Perhaps this title page and an imperfect recollection of the piece itself was the origin of Pope's ascribing the old K. John printed in 1591, to Shakespeare and Rowley. He remembered I suppose having seen these names coupled somewhere together, and forgot the particular piece. To the old K. John printed by S. Clarke in 1591 no author's name is prefixed. The greatest part of the present play was intended as verse, tho' the printer has, for the sake of saving a sheet or two of paper, exhibited it almost entirely as prose. Shakespeare, I am confident, wrote not a line of it. M."  Below this note, in different ink: "Wm. Rowley wrote for a different theatre. This piece was probably written about the year 1615."
