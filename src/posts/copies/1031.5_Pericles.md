A copy formerly in the possession of J. O. Halliwell[-Phillipps] now belongs to Mr. Perry, of Providence. The margins are much cut down, but the text is perfect and measures 6 15/16" x 4¾". The volume is bound by W. Pratt, and consists of thirty-four leaves. The title is defective.

Offered for sale by Quaritch in Autumn, 1995, catalogue, #41. From Quaritch catalogue: "Our copy belonged to James Orchard Halliwell-Phillipps (1820-1889), and bears his collational note at the end .... Our copy was ... in the Shakespearean Rarities catalogues of 1887 and 1891 (no. 63). This collection was sold en bloc to Marsden J. Perry of New York, and passed mainly to Henry Clay Folger in 1907. Pericles however found its way through a Sotheby sale (14-15 December 1906, lot 3017) to Quaritch, and from Quaritch via George D. Smith in 1918 to John L. Clawson of Buffalo (leather book-label, no. 717 in De Ricci's 1924 catalogue of the Clawson collection)." Purchased at Clawson sale by Gannon for $675.
