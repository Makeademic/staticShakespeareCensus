Clayton Mordaunt Cracherode, a trustee of the British Museum, and the owner who bequeathed the Folio to the Museum, was an avid book, print, coin, medal, and natural history collector. The majority of his life was spent seeking out new pieces for his collection, while remaining as insulated as possible. It would be wrong to say that Cracherode was a recluse, but he certainly had feelings of anxiety regarding travel, horses, and large crowds. His estate of Great Wymondley was held by a serjeantry that would have required him to serve as the cup-bearer in a coronation. For this reason Cracherode made a point of never visiting these lands and was subject to bouts of high anxiety whenever there were reports of George III’s ill health.
