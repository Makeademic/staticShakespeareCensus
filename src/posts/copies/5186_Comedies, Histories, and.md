This First Folio is among the best copies remaining in private hands. It was acquired in the late 18th century by James Caulfeild, 1st Earl of Charlemont and an Irish patriot. Remaining in the Charlemont library for decades, the volume narrowly escaped being destroyed with most of the rest of the library by a fire at Sotheby’s in 1865, when it was on loan with other rare Charlemont books. In 1951, this First Folio was displayed at an exhibit in the Galerie Nazarin at the Bibliothèque nationale de France that was described as 'the greatest assemblage of treasures of our literature and book-production that has ever been brought together.' It has been on deposit with the National Library of Scotland in Edinburgh from a private collection for over twenty years.
