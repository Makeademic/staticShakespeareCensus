This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

There is nearly no wear to the binding.

Owen D. Young (1874-1962), the second to last private owner of this copy, was a successful businessman and diplomat. He worked as general counsel for General Electric starting in 1912 and became president of the company in 1922 .... He also assisted in founding the National Broadcasting Corportation (NBC) and was the chairman of the Rockefeller Foundation from 1928 to 1939 .... He served as an advisor to five United States Presidents beginning with Woodrow Wilson and coauthored the Dawes Plan, which dealt with German reparations following the war.
