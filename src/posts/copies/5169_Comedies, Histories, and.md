This volume was apparently the first to permanently reside in Japan, having been purchased by Mr Kamijo in 1969. On his death in 1981, the Kamijo First Folio was passed on to Kamijo’s son, with the provision that access to it be proscribed for 13 years. It has remained sequestered and unseen ever since. Due to this, identification of the Kamijo First Folio has required some detective work. Another First Folio, the Daubuz copy, was listed as unfound as late as 1995. Jean Claude Daubuz, Esq., owned his First Folio during the first decades of the twentieth century. In a letter from April 1901, Daubuz described the volume as having been stained on "nearly every page ... in the upper corner with wine or some other red liquid." When the Daubuz copy was sold at auction in 1932, the Sotheby’s catalogue noted it as having "a pink stain caused by damp in the right hand top corner." A similar description appears in the Fletcher/Yushodo catalogue. The disappearance of the Daubuz First Folio and the presence of almost identical red or pink staining on the Kamijo copy seem to indicate that they are the same First Folio.
