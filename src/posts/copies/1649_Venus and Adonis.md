The British Museum copy was at one time the property of Thomas Jolley, F.S.A., the well-known collector in the early years of the nineteenth century. He stumbled upon it in one of his Lancashire rambles, in a volume which also contained the first edition of the Sonnets of 1609 and was purchased for\r\na few pence. At the sale of Jolley's library in 1844 it was bought by Thomas Grenville for £116 and bequeathed by him to the British Museum in 1846. It measures 6 13/16 x 4 7/8". The edges are somewhat closely cut, and some pages are slightly mended. It is bound in olive morocco by Clarke. It was reproduced by Mr. E. W. Ashbee in 1867, together with the edition of 1793.
