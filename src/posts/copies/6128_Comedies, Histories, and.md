To the Reader copied in ink at top of t.p.; "RR" and "D" in faint ink on verso of t.p.; flowery doodles along top and outer margin of *3r; page numbers added in early ink to the Catalogue; editorial notes at bottom of many pages noting obscure words on Temp, TGV, MWW, MfM, JC, Mac, Ham, Lear, Oth, A&C (stops halfway and no numeral at the end), e.g. on A1r, "unstanch" and "furze", or A2r "Bermoothes", and clarifying French words (in MWW), or correcting typos, showing comparison against some other text, and vertical lines marking line endings perhaps for versification. On p. 11 of Temp, suggests correcting Ferdinand's "I am, in my condition / A Prince (Miranda)" to "I am, Miranda, a Prince in my condition" for versification, an emendation not noted in Variorum Tempest (1892); suggests correcting "Quoth she before" to "Before (quoth she)" in Ham p. 293; suggests correcting "Norway himselfe, with terrible numbers" to "himself with numbers terrible" (following Pope or Capell?); corrects "wizard Sisters" in Mac 3.4 and 4.1 to "weyward" whereas F1 has "weyard"; corrects "our last, and least" in Lear p. 303 to "out last, not least" (following Pope?). Numerals at the of some plays: "19" on TGV; "32" on MWW; "47" on MfM, "100" on JC, "116" on Mac, "236" on Ham, "104" on Lear, "131" on Oth, ; also adds occasional SDs, as in Ham p. 301: "Trumpets sound, shot goes off". From auction catalogue: 'A1 "To the Reader" has small calligraphic 'T' written in a 16mm square in upper margin and two small pen-trials effaced in lower blank region ..."
