This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Originally bound in reverse (suede), dark brown calfskin, probably in the 1620s. Rebound, c.1890s, using reverse brown calfskin. The original leather front cover, which was worn and lacking the upper left surface corner, is inlayed. It bears the blind-stamped heraldic badge of Augustine Vincent (seriously worn). The original red-sprinkled edge-treatment is discernible, but no longer clear due to the unevenness of the edges.

This is first First Folio listed in the catalogue of the Folger Library, the largest holder of First Folios in the world. One of the original printers of the first Folger First Folio, William Jaggard, intended to present this copy to his friend Augustine Vincent.... An inscription by Vincent—'Ex Dono Willi Jaggard Typographi. AO 1623'—can still be read on signature A1+1. Almost three centuries later, in 1891, the first Folger First Folio was discovered in a private collection by the bookseller Alexander Balderston Railton...this copy was rebound in 1902—a fragment of Vincent’s ancient binding was incorporated into the upper cover—and then acquired, in 1903, by Henry Folger for the then record price of $48,000.
