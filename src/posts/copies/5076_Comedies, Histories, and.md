This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy is full of little hints of what might be earlier owners. However, the few pieces of information found in the volume are insufficient to make any real claims as to provenance. On A1 is found the manuscript annotation ‘Quod ero spero’ which is the motto of Henry Booth (1652-1693/94) and George Booth (1675-1758), first and second Earls of Warrington. It is also possible that the Latin motto, roughly translated as ‘I hope that I shall be’ or ‘What I shall be I hope’ could be a readers comment on the action of The Tempest. Another hint of an owner is the autograph on Y1v ‘Elizabeth Dagget.’ Once again though, it has proven impossible to trace this person due to the fact that the name is extremely common.
