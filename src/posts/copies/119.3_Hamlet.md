Most bibliographies consider Wing S2951 to be a variant issue of this edition (and date Wing S2951 to 1676 as well), but see Emma Depledge, "False Dating: The Case of the '1676' Hamlet Quartos", Papers of the Bibliographical Society of America, 2018. Numbered "5" in manuscript at head of title. From Maggs catalogue: "few stab holes at inner margin, a few marginal tears, minor ink blotting on the verso of the final leaf (not touching the text)." Bound in a late seventeenth-century volume containing: 1) Rochester's adaptation of Fletcher's Valentinian (1685, Wing F1354); 2) Otway's Alcibiades (1675, Wing O539); 3) Bankes's Destruction of Troy (1679, Wing B657); 4) Othello (1681, Wing S2940); 5) Hamlet (1676, Wing S2950); 6) Julius Caesar (1684, Wing S2922); 7) Settle's Cambyses (1675, Wing S2666); 8) Settle's Ibrahim the Illustrious Bassa (1677, Wing S2694); 9) Settle's Conquest of China (1676, Wing S2675); 10) Tate's adaptation of King Lear (1681, Wing S2918); 11) Lee's Tragedy of Nero (1675, Wing L883); 12) Dryden's Oedipus (1682, Wing D2323).
