Edmond Malone (1741-1812) was one of the great Shakespeare scholars and editors of his time. Part of his scholarly pursuit was the acquisition of early editions of various literary works for his research purposes. He considered his library to be a working library, rather than a show library, and as such the books were to be used extensively. His copy of the Folio reflects this in the numerous insertions made at the beginning of the volume. There are a total of 15 pre-original leaves that are mixed with the Folio preliminary leaves. These additional leaves contain engravings of buildings associated with Shakespeare, such as the Globe or New Place, various manuscript notations about the volume and First Folios in general, and engravings of Shakespeare and others associated with him.
