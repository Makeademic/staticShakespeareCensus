In this issue, a new bifolium A1.2 was added, including a resetting of the verses "To the Reader" in smaller type on A1v, to make room for the engraved portrait which was moved to that page, and a title page dated 1664 on A2r. The original A1 and A2, including the title page dated 1663, were apparently intended to be removed. In some copies the original was not removed and both title pages and "To the Reader" leaves, or some combination thereof, appear at some point in the volume, as noted under particular copies. This issue also added seven additional plays, which are listed on the 1664 title page. Copies with any of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: 1663 title leaf, with the portrait, 1663 "verses" leaf, 1664 title leaf, and the additional plays. On the 1663 title page, the edition statement (5th line of t.p.) is touched up with pen and ink; the leaf is patched in the lower and outer margins and is ruled in red, recto and verso; portrait in state 3. The 1663 "verses" leaf is extended and patched in the lower outer corner and margin and is ruled in red, recto and verso. The 1664 title leaf is bound after the other t.p.; backed. Imperfections affect text slightly on A3, 2X4 recto (catchword shaved), 3I3, [par.]C4, and [par.]F1 and very slightly on S5, 2X3, and 3I4. Variant states and printings, as recorded by James G. McManaway, "New discoveries in the Third Folio," PBSA, v. 70, (1976), 469-80, and other such variations, are identified in file.
