This copy contains several bookplates and signatures belonging to the Newdegate/Newdigate family. The earliest known owner was Sir Richard Newdegate, first Baronet of Arbury (1602-1678), who was a judge who so angered Cromwell with his impartiality that Cromwell had him thrown off the bench. Newdegate was rewarded for his services with a baronetcy by King Charles II in 1677...The volume remained in the Newdegate family until 1922 when it was sold by Sir Francis Alexander Newdigate Newdegate (1862-1936), who was the Governor of Tasmania and later Governor of Western Australia. This volume also contains a number of newly-discovered textual variants. On leaf tt1r in Othello there are six textual variants that were not known to previous scholars.
