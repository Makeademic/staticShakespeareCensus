This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

There is strong evidence to suggest that for a period of over twenty years this volume was the property of Lady Louisa Maddalena Lenox Tighe (1803-1900). Lady Louisa was the daughter of the fourth Duke of Richmond. When she was twelve years old she had the privilege of attending the ball held at the British Embassy in Brussels on the eve of the Battle of Waterloo .... After her death Woodstock [the estate] and the Folio passed to the next male relative Frederick Bunbury Tighe. In 1921 Woodstock was occupied by the Black and Tans and then, by the Free State Army until 1922. Apparently, the Folio had been removed from the house since it survived the burning of Woodstock by the IRA following the withdrawal of the troops.
