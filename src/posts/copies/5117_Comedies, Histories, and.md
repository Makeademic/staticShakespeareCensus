This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This volume had three consecutive owners of historical importance. The first was Godfrey Locker-Lampson (1875-1946), a Conservative MP who served as Aide-de-camp to Lt.-Gen. Henry Hughes Wilson and acted as a mediator between Wilson and Prime Minister David Lloyd George; he later served as a member of the British delegation to the League of Nations in 1928. The second was William Clarkson Van Antwerp (1867-1938) a major player on the New York Stock Exchange. Van Antwerp was one of the first to offer an inside perspective on the stock market crash of 1929 in his speech from 6 December of that year, "The Panic of 1929." The third was Harry Elkins Widener (1885-1912) who died when the Titanic sank in April 1912.
