These copies form a group that is sometimes referred to as F5, but they are better described as later states of F4. Presumably because the printer of the middle section of F4 (from King John through Romeo and Juliet) failed to provide the publishers with the correct number of sheets, in 1700 seventeen sheets (68 pages) were reprinted for inclusion alongside the remaining sheets. These newly printed sheets lack the boxed-rule lines on the sides and foot of the page.

This copy includes the title page with three publishers in the imprint (Wing S2915). Portrait in state 4. Contains reprinted sheets 2B3.4, 2I2.5, 2I3.4, 2N2.5, 2O3.4, 2T3.4, 2X3.4, 2Y1.6, 2Z2.5, 2Z3.4, *3B2.5, *3C2.5, *3C3.4, and *3E3.6. Imperfections affect text on 2B4 and 2D6, slightly on *3C5 verso, and very slightly on F1, K4, M5, 2B1,3, 2H6, *3E7, and 3R5. With Claude Dubosc’s engraving of the Shakespeare monument in Westminster Abbey, signed "P. Schemakers [sic] F. [i.e., by Peter Scheemakers, the sculptor of the monument] M.DCCXL.--Clau. Dubosc Sculp. in Whitecomb Street near Picadilly. ...", proof before lettering (Dubosc’s signature and address, if present, cropped); inserted (mounted) at end.

Annotated and emended in ink passim, chiefly in a late 17th-cent. hand likely that of Thomas Southerne--in Temp., Merry Wives, Errors, and Much Ado, in all Histories, and in all Tragedies, but in none of the additional plays.  Also marked passim with pairs of horizontal strokes in ink and in pencil.  Many marginalia are cropped. Annotations in this copy were cited as Southerne’s by John Payne Collier in his ed. of Shakespeare’s Works (1842-1844), passim (list on file)--cf. "The copy of the fourth folio (now the property of Mr. [William] Holgate), which once belonged to Southern, as is ascertained by his autograph, and various conjectural emendations in his hand-writing" (V, 71n)--and by Alexander Dyce in his eds. of Shakespeare’s Works (1857) and (1864-1867), passim (none other than those cited by Collier; lists on file). Annotated (front free endpaper verso): "Collated G. Croke.  Southern’s copy.  See his signature on the title page, mutilated by the binder.  The marginal marks = are also considered to be Southern’s" (19th cent.).
