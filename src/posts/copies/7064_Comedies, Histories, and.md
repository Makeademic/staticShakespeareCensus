In this issue, a new bifolium A1.2 was added, including a resetting of the verses "To the Reader" in smaller type on A1v, to make room for the engraved portrait which was moved to that page, and a title page dated 1664 on A2r. The original A1 and A2, including the title page dated 1663, were apparently intended to be removed. In some copies the original was not removed and both title pages and "To the Reader" leaves, or some combination thereof, appear at some point in the volume, as noted under particular copies. This issue also added seven additional plays, which are listed on the 1664 title page. Copies with any of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: 1664 title leaf, and the additional plays. The 1664 title leaf is patched at the inner edge and tail, affecting letterpress of title page, with the affected text supplied in manuscript facsim. or touched up with pen and ink.  The t.p. is red-ruled and the leaf mounted. Wants the associated "verses"; supplied is the corresponding leaf in the 4th Folio, [pi]1--the "verses" on the verso, with the port. above them in state 4.  The leaf is remargined in the upper and outer margins, and the page of "verses is red-ruled. Leaf πA3 is remargined (except at the tail), affecting letterpress on the verso, with the affected text supplied in manuscript facsim. Supplied from another copy are A1 or A6, perhaps 2M6, 2N1 (probably) or 2N6, 2X2 or 2X5, and [par.]G6.  Leaf [par.]G6 is patched, affecting text, with the affected text supplied in manuscript facsim. Imperfections affect text on T1, slightly on 2H5 and 2Q4, and very slightly on 12 other leaves (list on file). Variant states and printings, as recorded by James G. McManaway, "New discoveries in the Third Folio," PBSA, v. 70, (1976), 469-80, and other such variations, are identified in file.
