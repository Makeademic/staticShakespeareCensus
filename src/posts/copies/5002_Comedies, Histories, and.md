This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Lacks t.p. and preceding leaf with Ben Jonson's verses and sigs. E4, b6, c6, o3-4, aaa2; all these are supplied in facsimile. Sigs. c1 and x2-3 are believed to be from another copy. The leaf following the title is mounted, with margins repaired. Final leaf mutilated, without page number, and lacking lower half which bears colophon. Inserted at front: (1) Cutting from the Athenaeum and manuscript note by Edward Shillington about other copies of the first folio; (2) Correspondence from Sir George Grey relating to the purchase of this copy, dated 6 August 1892; (3) Correspondence from Mr R Garnett relating to the purchase of this copy, dated 17 May 1894; (4) Correspondence from bookseller Mr Bernard Quaritch relating to other copies of the first folio, dated 11 June 1894; (5) Correspondence from bookseller Mr Bernard Quaritch relating to the repair of this copy, dated 19 June 1894; (6) Page from the Athenaeum, dated 19 August 1899. The first paragraph of the article is pasted into the inside cover; (7) Off-print of an article by Professor S. Musgrove on a disputed reading in King Lear, dated May 1957.

This copy was given to the city of Auckland by Sir George Grey (1812–1898). Grey was a great explorer and instrumental in the colonization of both Australia and New Zealand. He was one of the leaders of two different expeditions that were the first Europeans to explore portions of Western and Northwestern Australia. During his diplomatic career he served as Governor of South Australia, twice as Governor of New Zealand, Governor of Cape Colony, South Africa, and Premier of New Zealand.
