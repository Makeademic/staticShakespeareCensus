This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Folger 73 is significant for the evidence it contains of the practice of book lending in England. The pastedown of this copy has the note ‘Lent a gentlema= ... [amount effaced] opon [sic] this Book. for. three Weeks. July. 18. :[i.e., 17]84:’ indicating that it was loaned out for a fee. Another copy at the Folger also bears the manuscript notations of a booklender. Folger 60 contains a note by Thomas Bourne who was a bookseller; in this copy the sum written is Bourne’s charge for lending a Folio, half the original wholesale cost of the volume, plus half the cost of the cheapest available binding. It would therefore make sense that the portions of the lender’s annotation that cannot be made out in this copy would dictate a similar fee for lending privileges.
