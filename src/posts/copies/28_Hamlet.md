BRITISH MUSEUM. 6 15/16 x 4 11/16". Title lacking; irregularly interleaved with text of modern edition of later version pasted on interleaves and also quotations from Theobald; a few variant readings on margins of text. "Bought 9 November 1858" stamped on I4 verso; crown stamp on B1 recto. Pasted on wrapper is the note: "This is the rare & most precious Edition of the Hamlet of 1603, of which the only other known copy is in the collection of the Duke of Devonshire. I purchased this volume of Messrs Boone in Sept. 1856 for £120. J. O. Halliwell." Inside cover is pasted this printed entry from catalogue: "No perfect copy is known of this, the original sketch of Shakespeare's Hamlet. All the older commentators were ignorant of its existence and it was not until the year 1825 that a copy, wanting the last leaf, was discovered. That copy, long supposed to be unique, is now in the possession of the Duke of Devonshire. The present one has the last leaf but wants the title. It is otherwise perfect in firm sound genuine condition throughout, and most of the leaves remarkably fine." The Rooney of Trinity College, Dublin (sold, 1856), Boone (sold, September, 1856), Halliwell (sold, November 9, 1858) copy. Kept in grey wrappers pasted over brown paper as at time of purchase, within red morocco case.
