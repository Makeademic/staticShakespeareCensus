This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This volume contains an unusual number of autographs and bookplates. The autographs that appear in the volume are as follows: John Cooper, W. Johnson of University College, Oxford, Howard Staunton (1810-1874), and William Jaggard (1868-1947). Howard Staunton was a respected Shakespeare scholar and a poem attributed to him appears in this volume with the title ‘On the First Folio’. The bookplates that appear are as follows: Sir Fulwar Skipwith (1676-1728), second Baronet, the Rev. John Day Collis D.D. (1816-1879), and Captain William Jaggard (1868-1947).
