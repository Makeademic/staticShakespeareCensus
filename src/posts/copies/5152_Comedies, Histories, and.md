This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The Society of Antiquaries of Scotland owned this copy prior to signing over much of their collection to the National Library of Scotland. The Society was founded in 1780 by David Steuart Erskine, eleventh Earl of Buchan, and it was granted a Royal Charter in 1783. Miss Clarke of Dunbar gave her copy of the Folio to the Society to supplement their growing collection in 1784. \r\n\r\nAs with so many copies the last few leaves of the volume are missing, specifically bbb5 and bbb6. A feature of this volume that stands out, is the cluster of state 1 variants found on ss3, ss3v, ss4, ss4v. While it is common to find multiple state 1 variants in a volume, it is rather uncommon to find so many in a tight cluster.
