This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be sold at his shop at the signe of the blacke Beare in Pauls Church-yard"; the original sheet πA2.5 was replaced by a cancel, which is printed on thicker paper and probably dates from 1641, after Thomas Cotes died. [DEEP]

In Folger copy 36, πA1 is wanting; supplied in facsim.  Leaf πA(πA2) (cancellans title leaf) is patched at the lower outer corner.  Leaf *3 is mutilated at the upper outer corner and in the outer margin.  Leaf 3d3 is patched at the lower outer corner, affecting rules on the recto and verso and the catchword on the recto (reproduced in facsim.).  Leaf 3d4 is patched passim, affecting the colophon ("s" of "Hawkins", reproduced in facsim.). Port. (on t.p) in state 3. Imperfections affect text very slightly on A3, C4, Q1, and Z4.
