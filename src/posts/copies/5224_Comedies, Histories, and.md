This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The binding is red goatskin. It has a gold-tooled border with fillets and the gold-tooled armorial stamp of George, Prince of Wales (when he was Prince Regent) on the front and back covers. There are gold-tooled lines along the edges of the binding and a gold-tooled border with fillets on the turn-ins. The binding is slightly worn and scuffed at the corners and on the top and bottom of the spine.

The Windsor First Folio was acquired around 1800 by the Prince of Wales, later George IV, for his personal library at Carlton House. An interesting feature of this volume is its binding and inlay of paper, which looks very similar to that of the Sir John Soane copy, though not as quite as well done and lacking all of the extra pages that are inserted in that copy. Also, like copy 94 in the Folger Shakespeare Library, one page of Romeo and Juliet (here ff1, there ee4) has extensive manuscript annotations.
