This issue lists H. Herringman, E. Brewster, and R. Bentley as publishers in the imprint.

Lacks π1 (replaced with mounted modern facsimile). A composite copy: leaves A1-C5, E2-2E6 from a copy with distinct wormhole pattern at lower outer corner; leaves C6-E1 (most of Merry Wives) are from another, unholed copy, and are heavily annotated in pencil, in part, at least, notes for production; thereafter the apportionment of leaves is not certain, though it appears that there is still alternation of the two copies, with a number of plays or groups of leaves annotated, in some cases rather lightly, in other cases as heavily and in the same hand as Merry Wives. Leaves A4, D2, k4,5, and Z4 are interpolated from yet other copies. The title leaf (π2) is backed, but has the same wormhole as succeeding leaves.

Title inscribed at head "Mr. Ch: Burton 1700"; woodcut bookplate, "Ex libris Gulielmus [sic] Jaggard." on front pastedown; 2 pictorial W. Jaggard bookplates on verso of front free endpaper, with typed address "Rose Bank, Stratford-on-Avon"; verso of ffe and recto of back free endpaper stamped "Shakespeare Press, Stratford-on-Avon" (i.e. W. Jaggard, after he set up that business in Stratford in 1909). A pencilled note on the free front endpaper above the bookplate (written ca. 1928 by the E. Thompson who also handled the sale of Jaggard's First Folio in that year) states that "this copy has been in possession of the Jaggard family for generations. It was used as a stage prompt copy during the eighteenth century and has many hundreds of interesting pencil annotations in the margins", though the annotations look closer to 19th c.; in 1972 correspondence concerning this copy (among gifts from the same donors), Michael Papantonio (Seven Gables Bookshop) remarked that "Jaggard was a trader in Shakespeare materials, bought and sold many copies, and made up copies; and the note that it was in the Jaggard family for generations is, I think at best pretty doubtful." Gift of William V.A. and Georgiana MacArthur Hansen in 1972, together with a copy of the Second Folio and the second quarto of Julius Caesar (1691); the Hansens had already donated, in 1971, a copy of the First Folio (cataloged as Hay Rare PR2751.A1x 1623). All three folios had previously been owned by Mrs. Hansen's father, Alfred MacArthur. See the Josiah record for the First Folio for further notes on the Hansens' connection to Brown University.
