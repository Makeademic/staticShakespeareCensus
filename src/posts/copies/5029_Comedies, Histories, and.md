This fragmented copy exists in two bound volumes, Comedies and Histories. The Comedies volume is incomplete, containing only nine of the fourteen comedies; it has six leaves of Romeo and Juliet, but no more material from the tragedies section .... Ownership of this copy can potentially be traced back to the 17th century, possibly to Edward Alleyn (1566–1626), the great Elizabethan actor, son-in-law of Philip Henslowe, the owner of the Rose Theatre. Alleyn founded Dulwich College in 1619. However, the First Folio is not mentioned in any of Alleyn’s documents nor is there any evidence of it among another bequest made to the college sixty years later, by William Cartwright (1606–1686). But Cartwright’s father was a friend of Alleyn who wrote the supposed November 1623 letter to the actor about having 'paid a sover-in for Shaksper’s booke of Plaies.' In 2003, the library was repairing another volume: the paste-down (now removed) of this volume contains part of a list of books. Included in the list is 'Shakspears Workes—folio'. The handwriting is the same as that found on Cartwright’s paintings. It can now be concluded that the Folio belonged to William Cartwright.
