This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The most distinctive feature of this copy is the transformation that the volume has undergone over the past decade. The current owner acquired the volume in 2002 and has since worked tirelessly to restore and improve the overall condition of the copy. Not only have damaged portions been repaired, but the owner has sought to replace facsimile leaves with originals. Replacing facsimile or damaged leaves was customary in the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. But in the twenty-first century few have been willing to devote the time and expense to tracking down individual leaves. At the time of viewing the Folio, only a few facsimile leaves still needed to be replaced. Although the owner accepts that it might not be possible to find the remaining leaves, he or she plans to continue to search.
