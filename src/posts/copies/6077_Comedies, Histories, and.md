This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be fold at the signe of the Blacke Beare" in "Pauls Church-yard" [DEEP]

Folger copy 3 is disbound and housed in two cases: in one case (37 cm.), the prelim. leaves through f5 as loose leaves, encapsulated; in the other case (33 cm.), f6 through the end.  The loose leaves are embrittled to the point of disintegration, affecting text; lesser embrittlement continues into the other portion.  Described in the 1890 octavo catalogue of the Warwick Castle Library Shakespeare Collection (Folger MS uncatalogued) as with "many pages very weak, and some broken". Port. (on title page) in state 3. Wanting leaves 2v2.5; leaves 2v3.4 duplicated. Damp-stained, 2x6 ff.  Described in the 1890 folio catalogue of the Warwick Castle Library Shakespeare Collection (Folger MS M.b. 31) as "wanting the whole of signature CCC," so evidently the present gathering 3C is supplied from another copy.

Described in Halliwell-Phillipps’s Hand-list of ... Shakespeariana (1862), #107: "This annotated folio belonged to the late Mr. [Samuel Weller] Singer, and was purchased by me at his sale at the low price of 19l., its curiosity having escaped the notice of the auctioneers.  Some of the old manuscript emendations are ingenious and perhaps sometimes right." The recto of leaf πA1 (with the so-called "verses" on the verso) is inscribed in a seventeenth-century hand with a list of books and/or authors and: "Sold the 14th = of February 1649; this booke, and I warrant it to be of the best addition, and perfectt; booke; sold by me SI Sarah Iones hir marke = widdow = at the whit = hors = book = seller in the littl = Britton, in london;"  This copy is described in the sale catalogue of the library of Samuel Weller Singer, III (Sotheby, May 16, 1860 ff.), #1474, as "wanting the verses", so this leaf evidently is supplied from another copy.
