The well publicized "discovery" of this Folio in 2004 was mired in controversy. Lilian Frances Cottle had died intestate and a two-year search for her nearest relative ensued. It was determined that the nearest living relative was her second cousin once-removed, Anne Humphries. However, Cottle’s stepdaughter, Maureen Cook, claimed that the volume was originally the property of her father’s family and that Cottle had died intestate intentionally so that the volume would go to her relatives rather than to Cook. Cook believed that the volume had belonged to her grandfather who was a valet with a particular fondness for books. In the end, Humphries was declared to be the rightful owner and she auctioned the volume in late 2004. Prior to the sale, Humphries was showered with media attention as the humble housewife who inherited a fortune in the form of a book from a relative she never knew she had. She made an appearance on the British television show Richard and Judy with Shakespearean actor Ben Chrystal and Anthony James West and was widely interviewed by print media outlets.
