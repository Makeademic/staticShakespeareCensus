The copies listed here cannot be identified with any one specific issue of the 1632 folio edition.

Hay Star copy: Imperfect: lacks leaves πA1-3; leaves πA2,3 (title, dedication) supplied from the 1807 E. and J. Wright type-facsimile reprint of the First Folio; title red-ruled, with "The second Impression" in ms. above the portrait, and the date altered to 1632 (though the anomalous Jaggard imprint is left unaltered); the plate has cut through the paper at some points. Leaf πA5 (conjugate with the missing title leaf), containing "Vpon the effigies of my worthy friend ...", etc., is in Todd's state Ib, indicating that the book was included in the first issue, though the original imprint of this copy, and its corresponding STC and ESTC entries, are indeterminate--see Todd. Leaves N3.4 (p. 149-152) are trimmed shorter at top and bottom, indicating that they may have been supplied from another copy.\r\n\tIn this copy, the signatures are as given by Greg for all copies, including none of his parenthesized variants except for the missigning of 2c2 as 2b2 and the omission of a signature on 2c3. (Note also that v1,2 are signed u1,2.) The pagination errors are as indicated for all copies, including none of the parenthesized variants.

A few early marginal x's in the text; otherwise without annotation except for the margin of p. 179 (P6r), which contains a number of alternate spellings of words from the text (e.g. "doe" for "do", "mercie" for "mercy") in an early italic hand, lightly trimmed.

Bound in early 19th-c. calf (presumably ca. 1808 or later, given the 1807 title leaf), rebacked; covers blind-tooled to a panel design, using small tools and floral roll tools; spine blind-tooled, lettered direct in early 19th-c. face, "Shakespeares comedies histories and tragedies / Second impression / Lond. 1632"; binder's endleaves are handmade laid, with fleur-de-lis/LVG watermark; marbled endleaves (simple comb pattern); all edges red.
