An odd quirk of this volume is the fact that Troilus and Cressida is placed after Timon of Athens and before Julius Caesar. This is somewhat strange since the volume was bound by Bedford, who had extensive experience with First Folios. Why a binder with such an intimate knowledge of this book would mistakenly bind Troilus and Cressida in the wrong spot is difficult to understand. One of the most logical explanations would be that an earlier binder had made the mistake and Bedford simply did not correct the mistake because the sewing of the volume was still fine and he simply felt it would be best not to re-sew it.
