This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be sold at his shop at the signe of the blacke Beare in Pauls Church yard," with the word "Pauls" in italics; the original sheet πA2.5 was replaced by a cancel, which is printed on thicker paper and probably dates from 1641, after Thomas Cotes died. [DEEP]

Folger copy 38 is nominally of STC 22274e.5. However, the issue cannot be definitively determined.  This copy contains πA(πA2) (cancellans title leaf), the recto of which (title page) reads consistently as of the third issue, but in place of its conjugate, πA(πA5) (cancellans "Effigies" leaf) proper to the third issue, this copy contains πA5 (cancellandum "Effigies" leaf) proper to the first issue (in state Ib); either (or both) of these leaves could be supplied from another copy. On πA(πA2) recto (title page), the first line of the title statement and the three lines of the imprint are touched up or supplied in pen-and-ink facsim.  In the imprint, the swash "R" of "Robert", recorded by Giles E. Dawson, TLS, Feb. 1, 1947, p. 65, is unquestionably supplied in manuscript and accordingly is not a press variant. Port. (on title page) in state 3. Leaves πA2-*1 have margins patched very unobtrusively and probably have adjacent text touched up with pen and ink. Leaves 3c3-5 are mutilated in the outer margin, affecting the catchword on 3c4 recto and some rules.  Wanting 3d4 (including the colophon); supplied in manuscript facsim. Leaf e6 (short and narrow and watermarked redundantly with e1) is supplied from another copy.  Either leaf f1 or f6 (watermarked redundantly) is supplied from another copy; probably f1 (touched up with pen and ink on the recto).  Leaf 2y6 (short, guarded) is probably supplied from another copy. Imperfections affect text very slightly on A3, E4, u6, and 2h5.
