This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy is one of three found in the first state, without Troilus and Cressida, although a copy of that play was added at a later date from a slightly smaller copy; the others in the first state are SC 5090 and SC 5146. Leaves K3.4 and perhaps t3.4 are supplied from another copy or copies. Text patched on V5 and 2d1 is completed in manuscript facsim. Text on i6, q6, 2d6, 2k1, 2t3, and 2v3 is touched up with pen and ink. Other imperfections affect text on D4, H6, b5, 2s4 and slightly affect text on 11 other leaves.

This copy is an example of the issue of the Folio in its first state. That is, the pages were gathered together and presumably sold before Troilus and Cressida was printed; therefore the play is missing entirely from the volume. The second issue contains the Romeo/Troilus leaf instead of the Prologue cancel leaf; the third issue is the standard issue, with the Prologue cancel leaf. In this particular copy Troilus was later inserted from a slightly smaller copy in an effort to perfect the text.
