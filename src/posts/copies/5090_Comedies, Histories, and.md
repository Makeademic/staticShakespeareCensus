This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

This copy is one of three found in the first state, without Troilus and Cressida, although a facsimile of that play was added at a later date; the others in the first state are SC 5103 and 5146.

Prior to Henry Folger’s acquisition of this copy it belonged to the Proprietary and Cottonian Libraries in Plymouth, England. These libraries formed part of the early foundation of the Plymouth Public Library system. This circumstance makes for a rather unusual situation in which it appears that, for a period of time, the First Folio might have actually been a regularly circulating volume in the library. A manuscript notation found above the Plymouth Public Library bookplate on the pastedown of the volume states ‘Not. To. Circulate | 7 Days.’
