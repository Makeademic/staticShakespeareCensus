The provenance history of this Folio is of interest due to the possibility that it has an unbroken chain of ownership that can be traced back to the original purchaser of the volume. Lee speculates in his Census that Thomas Arundell, second Baron Arundell of Wardour (1586–1643), purchased the volume shortly after its publication in 1623 .... A letter to Lee from George James Howard, ninth Earl of Carlisle (1843-1911), in 1902 sheds a bit more light on the original owner of this copy. In this letter he relates that the First Folio purchased by his ancestor Lord William Howard (1563-1640) ... had been bequeathed to another relative, Thomas Arundell, second Baron Arundell of Wardour, upon his death in 1640[.] This piece of information, while not absolute proof of the provenance of this volume, certainly lends strong weight to the claim that this Folio was in the continuous possession of one family until it was given to Stonyhurst College where it remains today.
