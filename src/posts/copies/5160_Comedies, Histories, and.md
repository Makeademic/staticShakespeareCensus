This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The cases have an acid free liner. The binding is reddish-brown (l*a*b = 58.97*10.02*30.02) buff leather with faint blind tooling. There are four raised bands on the spine.

It is possible that this volume was owned by Robert Wynn of Bodysgallen Hall near Llandudno, North Wales. This information is taken from the autograph that appears on the title page just below the portrait ‘Robert Wynn Bodescallan.’ Bodescallan is an alternate spelling of Bodysgallen. The exact dates of Wynn’s life are not known, however, he and his wife Katherine built the addition to Bodysgallen Hall, which bears the date 1620. It is also likely that Wynn is the same Robert Wynn who is memorialized in an elegy by Owen Griffydd in 1680.
