This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The volume is housed in red slipcase. The binding is red goatskin with gold tooling. The binding was done in the mid-nineteenth century (probably when it was acquired by John Jones). All page edges are gilt. There are five ribs on the spine. 'Shakespeare Comedies, Histories, & Tragedies' is written between the first and second ribs. 'London' appears at the very bottom of the spine below the fifth rib.

A striking aspect of this copy is the widespread manuscript annotations made by an owner in the late 17th or early 18th century. These annotations include a remarkable catalogue of plays that were either read or seen by this early owner, including works by John Dryden, Thomas Southerne, Nathaniel Lee, Nahum Tate, Elkanah Settle, Thomas Otway, works by Shakespeare not in the First Folio, John Crowne, Beaumont, Fletcher, and Margaret Cavendish …. It appears that the annotations date from sometime after the publication of the Beaumont and Fletcher Second Folio in 1679. There also appears to be a second hand widely annotating the volume in black ink …. This second annotator inserts dramatis personae for various plays throughout the volume.
