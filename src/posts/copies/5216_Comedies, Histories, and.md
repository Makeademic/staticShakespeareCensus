This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

In Act I of King John, words such as 'Cannon,' 'artillery,' and 'bullets' are marked by a red pen underscore. In Richard II, 2 Henry IV, 3 Henry VI, and Richard III someone has changed all of the colons to semicolons using red pen MS. The title-page of Titus Andronicus questions the play's authorship, ‘Not Shakespears; scarce a word’, as does the title-page of Cymbeline: ‘Not Shakespears, any part of it.’

\N

The binding is diced brown calf/goatskin with a tight figure eight gold-tooling border (the junctions of the figure eights are tiny six-point stars) surrounded by a solid rule gold-tooling border. The binding is very worn on the front and back covers with scuffs on both sides and bent corners...There is damage to the bottom of the binding. The National Art Library suggests that the binding dates from when the volume was acquired by John Forster.
