On verso of title page, Malone's note: "Bought from Mr Wagstaffe bookseller in Febr 1779, at the exorbitant price of Two Guineas. / This is the only copy that I have seen, except that in the possession of Mr Steevens; and perhaps these are the only two now extant. Mr Capel for 30 years searched for one in vain. / This play was exhibited in or before 1594, for on the Stationers' Books is the following Entry: / May 2/ 1594. / Peter Shorte.] A pleasaunt conceipted hystorie called the Taming of a Shrowe. / E. M."; Later note by Malone: "An earlier edition of this play was discovered in the Duke of Bridgewaters Library in 1802, printed in 1596; and another was purchased at a sale by the Duke of Roxburgh about the same time. I have collated this copy with the edn. of 1596, and marked the variations. / E Malone."
