This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

According to the Lee census, this copy has "fly-leaf in facsimile; title in facsimile with original portrait; preliminary leaf ('To the great Variety of Readers') partly in facsimile; other preliminary leaves and last leaf inlaid; many margins restored and a few pages mended."

T. Harrison Garrett (1849-1888), an owner of this copy, led a brief but interesting life. At the age of twelve Garrett twice ran away from home and unsuccessfully tried to join the Confederate Army, but was denied entrance. At the precocious age of 14 he started his college career at Princeton where he began his lifelong passion for collecting rare books and rare coins. According to notes found in the volume, Garrett acquired his copy of the First Folio when he was only fifteen years old.
