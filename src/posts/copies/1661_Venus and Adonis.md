The Bodleian copy of 1602 (8°. M 9, Art B S) bears the autograph signature of Robert Burton. It has been in the Library since 1640, when it was forwarded in conformity with the clause of Burton's will: 'If I have any books the University Library hath not, let them take them.' This copy was the first edition of the poem to pass the portals of the Bodleian Library. That Burton was well acquainted with Venus and Adonis is clear from a mnemonic quotation of four lines in his Anatomy of Melancholy (1621). Burton's copy is now bound up with five other tracts, only one of which was his property. The Venus comes second in the volume. Some of the leaves are uncut. The measurements are 5 9/16 x 3 3/8".
