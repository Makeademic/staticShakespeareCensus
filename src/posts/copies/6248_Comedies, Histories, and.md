This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be sold at his shop at the signe of the blacke Beare in Pauls Church yard," with the word "Pauls" in italics; the original sheet πA2.5 was replaced by a cancel, which is printed on thicker paper and probably dates from 1641, after Thomas Cotes died. [DEEP]

Accompanying documents: Yushodo catalogue citing Buddenbrooks, Inc., Boston: "The “Effigies” leaf is in expert facsimile, and five other leaves of the initial gathering have been expertly and unobtrusively remargined or relaid; the frontispiece verses, Ben Jonson’s “To the Reader,” have approximately 14 words supplied in an early hand in pen-and-ink facsimile, in near perfect duplication of the printing; *3 and 4 were supplied from another copy and have been extended; a few leaves at the beginning of the Comedies are remargined at the top, with a few parts of rules expertly supplied in pen-and-ink; the final leaf has been inlaid with a few rules supplied in pen-and-ink."
