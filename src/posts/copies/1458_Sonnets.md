When he returned it he made this drawing. I was confined by a sore throat, and was attended by Mr. Atkinson, the apocathary, of whom the above figure whom Shakespeare addresses, is a caricature. E.M." Shakespeare is saying: "If thou couldst, Doctor, cast / The water of my sonnets, find their disease, / Or purge my editor, till he understood them, / I would applaud thee,  &c."

Of the two copies in the Bodleian Library, the one which is reproduced here belongs to the Malone collection and is bound up with the first edition of Lucrece. It has the Aspley imprint, and measures 7 5/16 x 5 1/16", being inlaid on paper measuring 9 1/8 x 7 1/8". Malone acquired the volume in April, 1779, paying twenty guineas for the two quartos. He lent the volume to Steevens in the same year. Malone subsequently inlaid and bound up the two tracts with quarto editions of Hamlet (1607), of Lovers Labour's Lost (1598), of Pericles (1609 and 1619), and A Yorkshire Tragedy (1608). The whole volume was labelled 'Shakespeare Old Quartos, Vol. III.' It is now numbered Malone 34.
