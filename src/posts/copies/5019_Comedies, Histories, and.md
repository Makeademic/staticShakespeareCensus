Doctor George Thackeray, Provost of King’s College, owned this First Folio from 1814 until his death in 1850. He is the only recorded private owner. Thackeray was a well-known book-collector and connoisseur of good bindings. He bequeathed his library, including his First Folio, to King’s College. Although the volume has been with the college ever since, Sidney Lee omitted it from his 1902 Census 'by some unaccountable oversight.' Lee was informed of the First Folio’s existence in a letter dated 19 June 1924 by the renowned economist John Maynard Keynes, who was a fellow of King’s College.
