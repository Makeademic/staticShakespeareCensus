In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

The Boston Public Library’s Folio is set apart by its virtually unmarred text. While the marginal repairs are numerous, they are very minor. In the mid-1840s it was submitted for a cleaning by the first owner listed in the provenance, the London bookseller Thomas Rodd. But Rodd hated 'washed' books, preferring to 'see a book black as the ground than after its undergoing the ordeal of infernal wash tubs and lies.'
