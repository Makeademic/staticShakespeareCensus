This copy has a long history with the Catholic Church. The first known owner of the volume was John Milner (1752-1826), vicar apostolic of the midland district and writer. Milner was famous for his vehement disapproval of liberal Catholics and of any semblance of compromise on Catholic doctrine. Some of his most famous writings were The History, Civil and Ecclesiastical, and Survey of the Antiquities of Winchester, published in two volumes between 1798 and 1801, and The End of Religious Controversy, published in 1818. Following Milner’s death the volume was housed in a Roman Catholic clergy-house in Winchester until it was acquired by John Vertue (1826-1900). Vertue was the first Catholic bishop of Portsmouth when the diocese of Portsmouth was created in 1882.
