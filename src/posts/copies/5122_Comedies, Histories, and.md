Known as the Bridgewater copy, this Folio’s provenance can be traced all the way back to the year of its publication in 1623; its first owner, lawyer and politician, John Egerton, 1st Earl of Bridgewater, may have purchased the copy the same year since the binding is from the early 17th century...After the 3rd, and final, Earl’s death, the Folio and other trust property was passed down to the Earls of Ellesmere: another branch of the Egerton clan who took up residence at Bridgewater House, London. While in the Ellesmere’s possession, John Payne Collier used the Bridgewater copy to create his first edition of Shakespeare (1822-44), forging supposed 'contemporary' emendations in the Folio.
