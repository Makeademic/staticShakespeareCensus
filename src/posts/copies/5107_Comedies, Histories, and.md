This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

Quires A and B are from another copy and are heavily annotated. Brown ink MS notes at the start of plays that were printed for the first time in the First Folio say something like, "This is the first known edition of this play." The (often cropped) inscriptions with the initials "JB" are from Joseph Batailhey; some are dated Aug. 1698 and one is dated 1701. Macbeth and Hamlet have some notes comparing the text to the Second Folio in both black and brown ink...Cymbeline has MS editorial notes like quires A and B, but they are not as frequent and are in pencil instead of pen.

\N

Bound in the eighteenth century in ¼ brown stained calfskin spine with later(?) blue paper sides with vellum edging underneath. Sewn on 5 raised cords, laced through the boards. Front bead on a core made rolled paper sewn with red and white thread. There is no edge treatment. Endpapers are nonpareil marble paper pattern in blue, green yellow and red color.
