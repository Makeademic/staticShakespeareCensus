This copy—which was used as the basis for Yale’s facsimile edition of the Folio—was sold c. 1792 by bookseller, Thomas Payne ... to Henry Constantine Jennings for 70 guineas .... George Hibbert of Portland Place, London, acquired the copy c. 1819. A merchant, Member of Parliament for Seaford, Sussex, and founder of the London Institution—the forerunner to the University of London—Hibbert was keenly interested in art and books; he owned a Gutenberg Bible and edited Caxton's translation of Ovid, Six Books of Metamorphoses in 1819.... It appears from the date of the next sale of the four folios by Sotheby’s—on 12 March 1847—that John Wilks II was the owner of the copy; he died of swollen tonsils on 24 January 1846.  While Wilks Jr. served as a Member of Parliament for Sudbury, he was best known as the notorious 'Bubble Wilks,' who cheated investors in various mining companies and who embezzled from churches before being effectively exiled from Britain by his family. At the 1847 Sotheby’s sale, Wilks’ folios went for £155 to John Dunn Gardner—the Conservative MP and illegitimate son of brewer, John Margetts (who called himself the Earl of Leicester) and Sarah Dunn Gardner (Marchioness Townshend)..... The three folios were again auctioned en bloc by Sotheby’s on 6 July 1854, with bookseller Joseph Lilly of Covent Garden, paying out £250. He, in turn, sold them to Henry Huth—an ardent traveler and collector who used Lilly’s talents to help him build his library.... Alfred Henry Huth, Henry’s second son and a fellow bibliophile, took possession of the folios...Sotheby’s hosted the seven-day Huth sale, but on 11 November 1911, before the sale began, New York businessman, Alexander Smith Cochran, purchased the entire Shakespeare collection for £40,000. Cochran, a graduate of Yale University (B.A. 1896), founded the school’s Elizabethan Club—a social club which featured a collection of 16th- and 17th-century texts—in December 1911, donating the folios from the Huth sale, and more than one hundred Elizabethan and Jacobean books and manuscripts.
