This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

It has been theorized in the past that several leaves in this volume were inserted from R&W #165. While this copy certainly has leaves from another copy inserted there is no evidence to support the notion that the copy discussed in Lee as having been purchased by Lenox c. 1855 that was used to perfect 167 is the same as copy 165 .... It has proven impossible to confirm Lee’s statement that John Lichfield described this copy in the Cabinet or Monthly Report of Polite Literature in 1807. There were two editions of the publication that year and Lichfield does not appear in either of them. Since this is the only connection that existed between this copy and Lichfield it is uncertain whether he was actually an owner.
