This volume is notable for containing only one of only five extant impressions of the portrait in its first state. "Acquired c 1820 by John Delafield Phelps, of Lincoln’s Inn, on whose death in Dec. 1842 it passed to his nephew, William John Phelps, who, dying in Sept. 1881, was succeeded in his property by his nephew, the present owner" (Lee, Census, 1902). The ownership history of the volume since Lee’s Census in 1902 is slightly muddled but can be roughly pieced together from Sotheby’s and Quaritch’s records. It seems that upon the death of William Phelps, Esq. the volume passed to Captain A.W. Clifford, who is presumed to have been his heir since he had the same address. It was purchased by Quaritch for £4,200 at Clifford’s sale, Sotheby’s 449, 18 April 1921. Following this Charles Edward Barry Young (1850–1928) of Kingham, Oxfordshire, offered to assist in purchasing the copy for the British Museum. Young ultimately paid £10,000 and the Museum’s trustees paid £1,900. The volume was received into the Museum collection by A. W. Pollard in Sept. 1922.
