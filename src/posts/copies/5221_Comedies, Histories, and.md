This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

It is likely that the earliest owner known owner of this copy was one of two men bearing the name of Edward, Lord Suffield, either Edward Harbord (1781-1835), third Baron Suffield or Edward Vernon Harbord (1813-1853), fourth Baron Suffield. This is due to the appearance of the bookplate reading "Edward Lord Suffield" found on the pastedown of the volume. The autograph ‘May 29th, 1858 Suffield’ found on V1 indicates that the Folio remained in the family in 1858 in the possession of Charles Harbord, fifth Baron Suffield (1830-1914). It then would have passed to his son, Charles Harbord, sixth Baron Suffield (1855-1924). His son, Victor Alexander Charles Harbord, seventh Baron Suffield (1897-1943), then inherited it and sold the volume at Sotheby’s 765, 29 June 1938.
