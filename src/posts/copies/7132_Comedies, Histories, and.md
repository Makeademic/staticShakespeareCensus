In this issue, a new bifolium A1.2 was added, including a resetting of the verses "To the Reader" in smaller type on A1v, to make room for the engraved portrait which was moved to that page, and a title page dated 1664 on A2r. The original A1 and A2, including the title page dated 1663, were apparently intended to be removed. In some copies the original was not removed and both title pages and "To the Reader" leaves, or some combination thereof, appear at some point in the volume, as noted under particular copies. This issue also added seven additional plays, which are listed on the 1664 title page. Copies with any of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: no "verses" leaf, no title page, but with the additional plays. From auction catalogue: "title, portrait, other preliminary leaves, and the last 7 leaves all supplied in facsimile (along with A1, C1, F1, I6, K4, Q3, X3, X4, Bb4, Bb5, Ee2, Gg1, Gg2, Gg5, Hh2, Oo3, Vv4-6, Xx3, Fff1, Fff3-4, Fff6, Hhh4, Nnn1, Rrr4, Eeee2-4, and in added plays a2-5), several leaves towards the end (Sir John Oldcastle and the Puritan Widow) apparently inserted from another copy, and with a few small wormholes or other damage affecting letters, B3 and Qqq3 with corner torn away (the first restored with text in facsimile), 0o4-5 partially adhered, short tears in Ooo1, Ppp6, and Qqq6, fore-margin of Rr3 strengthened, small burnholes in Q1, Tt5, Ddd5, a very few rule-borders cropped or with headlines shaved, some slight discoloration throughout"
