The copies listed here cannot be identified with any one specific issue of the 1632 folio edition.

Folger copy 32 is a doctored copy.  It is nominally of the first issue, as formulated by William B. Todd, "Issues and states of the Second Folio and Milton’s epitaph on Shakespeare," Studies in bibliography, v. 5 (1952-53), p. 81-108.  However, the issue cannot be definitively determined.  This copy wants πA2 (title leaf) but contains πA5 ("Effigies" leaf) proper to the first issue (in state Ib). Wants port. (on title page). Wants also πA1,3,4.  Supplied in the place of πA1,2(title leaf),3,4 are facsims. of the leaves bearing the corresponding text in the 1st Folio, respectively πA1, πA1+1 (title leaf; with facsim. port. on a separate piece pasted on), πA2, and πA3. Wants also 3d4 (including the colophon); supplied is a facsim. of the leaf bearing the corresponding text in the 1st Folio (3b6). Leaves supplied from another copy are: I3.4, distinctively stained and (I3 recto) atypically emended in manuscript; a3, with a watermark that redounds that in a4 and atypically stained; l1, with a watermark that redounds that in l6, short, and distinctively soiled; l2, with a watermark that redounds that in l5 and distinctively soiled; 2o1.6, narrow and distinctively soiled; 2r3, with a watermark that redounds that in 2r4, short, and distinctively soiled; 2y6, short, narrow, and distinctively soiled; and 3a3-3d3, distinctively stained.  Other leaves, the origin of which is questionable (likely supplied from another copy but possibly proper to this copy but damaged because once loose and protruding) are: I1,2,5,6; S4; V1-6; X1-6; 2B2-6; a1,2,4-6; f1-6; and t2-5. Imperfections affect text on 3d3 and very slightly on 11 other leaves (list on file). "Notes and Corrections in Second Folio" (i.e., this copy), typescript, 2 unnumb. leaves, removed to file.
