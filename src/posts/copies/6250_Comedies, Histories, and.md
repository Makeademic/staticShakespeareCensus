This issue lists Robert Allot as the publisher in the imprint and lists it as "to be sold at his shop at the signe of the blacke Beare in Pauls Church yard," with the word "Pauls" in italics; the original sheet πA2.5 was replaced by a cancel, which is printed on thicker paper and probably dates from 1641, after Thomas Cotes died. [DEEP]

Bliss-Lenox Copy. Two title pages present in volume, a loose t.p. of Todd's state Ia laid in, but the bound in copy is state III. Ms. insert from a previous librarian notes variants in paging binding order.

The remainder of the passage was cut off when rebound,  3)In All's Well (230) marks with an x "He was famous sir in his profession..." and includes (again in the bottom margin) a line about Democritus attributed to the Roman philosopher Celsus: "vir jure, magni nominis Celsus de Domocritu"  [justly a man of great reputation], and 4) in Midsummer (149) notes lines that are "a complement to Queen Elizabeth" and at (159) noting the line from Lysander, "The thrice three Muses, mourning for the death of learning, late deceast in beggary," the reader adds: "Spencer, as I conjecture, is the person hinted at, who died 1598 very poor". Other smaller corrections throughout.
