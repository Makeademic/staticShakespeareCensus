This edition exists in three distinct states: the first includes copies that were sold without Troilus and Cressida; the second contains Troilus and Cressida but without its prologue and with a redundant final page of Romeo and Juliet crossed out by the printer; the third includes a cancel leaf with the Prologue of Troilus on its recto and the first page of the play, reprinted from a new setting of type, on its verso. In none of the states is Troilus included in the "Catalogue" of plays.

It is possible that this Folio was owned by Edward Scarisbrick (1637-1709), and at the very least, it was used enough by him that he chose to leave an autograph in the volume. Scarisbrick was a Jesuit who was accused by Titus Oates of being part of a Catholic plot to assassinate Charles II...Scarisbrick, who had fled to France where he had been educated, was able to return to England in 1693 and took a position as chaplain to the Culcheth family in Lancashire. He remained in that post until his death in 1709.
