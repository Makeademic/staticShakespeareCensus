Described as one of the very best copies extant, this First Folio has had a rich history. It was owned by James Boaden (1762-1839), the playwright most famous for his adaptations of works by Ann Radcliffe. Boaden was caught up in the controversy surrounding Samuel Ireland and the Shakespeare forgeries he had produced .... After this copy had arrived in America, Rosenbach held it in his famous New York vault, determined not to sell it except as part of his entire collection.  When Rosenbach eventually decided to sell his books, in 1951, the volume was acquired by Martin Bodmer (1899-1971), a Swiss collector from Zurich and vice-president of the International Red Cross.  In contrast to its covert departure from London, the soon-to-be Geneva First Folio was given a glittery, celebrity-studded going away party attended by Margaret Truman, among others.  The party’s central event was the presentation of a cake decorated to resemble a First Folio.
