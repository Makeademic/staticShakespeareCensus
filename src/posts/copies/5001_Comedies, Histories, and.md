The earliest known owner of this copy was Charles Howard, Eleventh Duke of Norfolk. After his wife was declared insane, Charles entered into a series of long-term extramarital relationships with women such as Shakespearean actress Charlotte Tidswell, and Mary Gibbon — with whom he had six children .... Because the eleventh duke left no legitimate heir, the dukedom passed to his third cousin, Bernard Edward Howard, though Charles Howard’s library did not. The twelfth Duke of Norfolk was obliged to buy his predecessor’s books from the executors. \r\n\r\nThe folio was passed down from duke to duke, but at some point, the volume was mislabeled as a Third Folio, so that on 13 April 1902, the Controller of the Household at Arundel Castle wrote to Sidney Lee, informing him 'that this is not a copy of the 1st Folio.' However, Henry Fitzalan-Howard, Fifteenth Duke of Norfolk, sent the copy to the British Museum so that Lee could examine the text—it was a First Folio after all.\r\n\r\nThe library of the Duke of Norfolk was begun by Edward Howard (1686–1777), the 9th Duke. The Folio contains the bookplate of Bernard Edward Howard, K. G. (1765–1842), 12th Duke. Letters from the Duke of Norfolk’s Librarian (6 and 27 June 2001) say that Charles Howard, K. G. (1746–1815), 11th Duke, was ‘a discerning bibliophile’ and widely educated; ‘recent research has shown that the books in the library with the 12th Duke’s bookplate were all acquired by the 11th Duke. As they were not entailed, the 12th Duke had to buy them from the executors, and he put his bookplate into them at that time'. There appears to be no record in the archives of where or when the 11th Duke bought the Folio, but the purchase was probably between about 1790 and 1815. Owned since by the successive Dukes of Norfolk. The owner now is Edward Fitzalan-Howard, (b. 1956), 18th Duke of Norfolk.
