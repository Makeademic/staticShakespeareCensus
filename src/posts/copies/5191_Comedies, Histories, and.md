In 1964, Pope Paul VI stole this copy of the First Folio. It was, by all accounts, an honest mistake on the pope’s part. Three members of the Royal Shakespeare Company, Dorothy Tutin, Derek Godfrey, and Tony Church had come to Rome to perform a recital in the Palazzo Pio before the pope .... The actors had brought along the RSC’s prized copy of the First Folio so that the pope could bless it at the conclusion of their performance. However, the pontiff had not been adequately briefed and accepted it as a gift, 'almost causing an international incident,' according to the RSC. The volume apparently remained in the Vatican’s library for some time before diplomatic negotiations succeeded in getting the folio returned to England, presumably unblessed.
