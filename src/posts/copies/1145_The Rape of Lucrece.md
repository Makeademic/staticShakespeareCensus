Two fine copies are now in America. One of these belongs  to Mr. William Augustus White, of Brooklyn. Mr. White's copy, which measures 7 1/16 x 5 3/8", seems to have been at the beginning of the nineteenth century in the Chapter library of Lincoln Cathedral. It subsequently passed into the possession of Sir William Bolland, Baron of the Exchequer, who died in 1840. On Sir William Bolland's death, it appears to have been purchased by the well-known bookseller, Thomas \r\nRodd, for 100 guineas. It then passed into the library of Frederick Perkins, of Chipstead (1780-1860). At the sale of Perkins' library on July 10, 1889, when the catalogue noticed a small hole burnt in two leaves, destroying a few letters , it was purchased by Mr. Bernard Quaritch, the London bookseller, for £200, and was acquired by the present owner.
