The copies listed here cannot be identified with any one specific issue of the 1632 folio edition.

Folger copy 29 is nominally of the first issue, as formulated by William B. Todd, "Issues and states of the Second Folio and Milton’s epitaph on Shakespeare," Studies in bibliography, v. 5 (1952-53), p. 81-108.  However, the issue cannot be definitively determined.  This copy wants πA2 (title leaf) but contains πA5 ("Effigies" leaf) proper to the first issue (in state Ib). Wants port. (on title page). Wants also πA1,3.  Leaves πA4-6 are misbound between *1 and *2. Wants also 3d3,4 (including the colophon); supplied are the leaves bearing the corresponding text in the 3rd Folio (4E3,4). Leaves a2.5 are duplicated, one copy bound inside the other. Imperfections affect text slightly on S4 and very slightly on B6, E3, P1, Z4, and e3.

Manuscript additions include the name (18th-cent.) "John Lloyd" (πA4 recto) and the signature (17th-cent.) of Charles Rees (I5 recto, 2C2 verso, and b6 recto). Inscribed (front pastedown): "a present from Edward Holt Esquire, September 1804".  This inscription continues:  "the deficiency of two leaves at the end of this book is supplied from Dean’s Edition of Cymbeline [Manchester: R. & W. Dean & Co., 1802], affixed within the [rear] cover" (of which only traces remain).  Other inscriptions are obliterated (πA4 recto and A1 recto). Book label, with motto "Qui Corvos Pascit Pascere Potest Grues" surrounding the letter "H", presumably of Edward Holt.
