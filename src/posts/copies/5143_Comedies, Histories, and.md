Following the drowning death of Caleb Fiske Harris (1818-1881), an owner of this copy, and his wife, this Folio, along with the rest of Harris’ collection, was placed in the hands of Sidney S. Rider. In 1882 Harris’ cousin, Senator Henry Bowen Anthony (1815-1884), negotiated to purchase the entire collection from Rider. However, it was discovered that many key volumes from the collection were missing, including the First Folio. Rider was a bookseller who was notorious for his inscrutable practices, and it was never known what actually happened to the Folio. It seems likely that Rider saw an opportunity to make a personal profit and sold the volume to the extremely wealthy Astor family, rather than to an unnamed individual in Kentucky.
