This issue is dated 1663, with verses "To the Reader" on A1v and the title page on [A2]r. Some copies include the portrait on the title page and some do not. Only copies with none of the distinctive features of the 1664 issue are listed here.

Includes: no title leaf, no "verses" leaf, no additional plays. Supplied are a facsimile of the 1663 "verses" leaf (-πA1) and the 1663 title leaf (-πA2). Contains many leaves throughout that, while undoubtedly genuine, are short or narrow or both; most such leaves are remargined at the head or the outer margin, or both.  Other leaves are further mutilated and patched or remargined--πA3 at the lower outer corner and inner margin also, πA4 at the inner margin also, 2O6 at the lower outer corner also, and 4E3 at the lower outer corner and inner margin also.  Leaf 4E4 is mutilated at the head, inner margin, and lower outer corner and is mounted. The first line of πA3, recto and verso, and the headline of 4D2,3 and 4E3, recto and verso, are completed in manuscript facsim. Imperfections affect text slightly on 3A2 and very slightly on 11 other leaves; list on file. Variant states and printings, as recorded by James G. McManaway, "New discoveries in the Third Folio," PBSA, v. 70, (1976), 469-80, and other such variations, are identified in file.
