Came to the University as part of the “Bibliothek des Frey-Grynaeischen Instituts”. The Frey-Grynaean Institute was founded by Johann Ludwig Frey, whose great-grandfather, one Johann Jakob Frey (1606–1636), spent a considerable amount of his life in England and from whose possessions the Second Folio came to Basel. It is unclear whether the ex libris “EX. BIBLIOTH. IOH. IACOBI FREY. BASIL.” on the inside of the Folio’s cover refers to the original Johann Jakob Frey or his son of the same name who inherited his father’s collection. Johann Ludwig Frey inherited the collections left to him by his father including the Second Folio and added them to the library in the Frey-Grynaean Institute. In 1909 the Second Folio as rediscovered when a large part of the Frey-Grynaean library collection were transferred to the University Library for security, among other reasons.
